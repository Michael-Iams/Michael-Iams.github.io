Obituary
Bertine Oline Syverson was the fourth of five children born to parents Ole and Anna Botolvsdatter Larson Syverson. She was born in Olmsted County, MN, close to Canisteo, Dodge Cty, MN where her maternal grandparents and their younger children had arrived about 1854, after a stay in Madison, WI and then 2 years in NE Iowa as new immigrants from Norway. Both of Bertha's parents were immigrants: her mother, Anna, age 13 in 1851 with her siblings and parents, as well as cousins, aunts and uncles. (Anna's mother's sister was my g.g.grandmother, Kari Hansdatter Svardal Fjerstad; Kari and her family were on the ship with Bertine's mother, her mother's siblings, parents etc.).

Bertine's parents moved to Nevada, Mower County, close to where some of her mother's other siblings also lived, about 1875. 

In 1879 Bertine's parents moved to Riverton Township, Clay County, MN where they got a homestead as well as purchased additional land, and were farming the remainder of their lives. 

Bertine, like many children of Norwegian immigrants was at times called a variety of names, such as 'Bertie', 'Lena' or 'Lina', short for 'Oline' her second name; 'Bertha' - the Americanized version of her first name, etc. Her father refers to her as 'Lina' in a couple of the documents; census records show the variety.

Bertine married Corbley Iams in 1895 and they had two sons, Orvis and Victor. Bertina lived in ND, Montana and Arizona most of her adult life. She died in a rest home in Missoula, MT.
