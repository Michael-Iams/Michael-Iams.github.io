    John Ijams, s/o of Capt John Ijams & Rebecca Jones, was born about 1755. On the plantation next to his father's lived the Howard family. John had an affair with a slave woman on the Howard plantation as documented in Carmen Harleston's family history. From this union, a son was born who had a light complexion.     His father, John, a wealthy merchant and a member of Old South River Club, was styled Captain because of service in the colonial militia. Took the Oath of Allegiance before Hon. Richard Harwood, Jr. on March 1, 1778.



    John IJAMS, one of the older sons of Captain John IJAMS and Rebecca JONES his wife, was born in All Hallow's Parish. At the outbreak of the Revolution John Weems on September 16, 1776, recommended him for Ensign of a company to be raised in Anne Arundel County. The next day the Council of Safety appointed him Ensign as John IJAMS Jr. He was soon transferred to a Company of Matrosses and assigned as Ensign and was frequently styled Third Lieutenant. On May 31, 1777, he resigned his commission as Third Lieutenant of the Artillery Company. On March 2, 1778, he reentered the service and was appointed Second Lieutenant of the West River Battalion of Militia. Later he was advanced to Captain and on February 10, 1780, received $1,350.00 for recruiting service. Likewise on July 13, 1781, Captain John IJAMS received additional allotment for recruiting service.


    Matross -- a onetime gunner's mate (as during the American Revolution) that assisted in loading and firing and sponging guns

    FRIDAY, October 25, 1776.

    On motion of Mr. J. Hall, Resolved, That the council of safety
    be authorised and empowered to make out commissions for captain
    Thomas Watkins, and also for his first and second lieutenants and
    ensign, Levin Lawrence, Thomas Todd, and John Iiams, jun., as
    as captain, first, second, and third lieutenants of a company of
    matrosses to be stationed in the city of Annapolis, and that they
    or either of them have power to enlist all the men of said company
    that are willing to enlist as matrosses, and to discharge such of
    them as are not willing to enlist after the first of December, and
    enlist others, so that the said company may be completed to the
    number of ninety-two ptivates, besides commissioned and non- 
    commissioned officers.
    Convention adjourns till Monday morning, 9 o'clock.
