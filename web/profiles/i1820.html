    Howard Lecky - In one particular case, while I was researching some
    information, I found and entry that I didn't believe was correct. Jimmy Meighen had
    done the research for Howard Lecky for this family line. The person in
    question was Mercy Walton who had married a Thomas Iiams. Everyone reading this
    entry in Lecky's "In Mile Country and Its Pioneer Families" took this entry as
    correct. In doing some extended research in Ft. Wayne, Indiana we came
    across the following.
    In the genealogy by Howard Lecky -"Tenmile Country and Its Pioneer People",
    he indicates that this Thomas married "Mercia Walton". We located the
    genealogy on the Walton family that resided in Washington County, Pennsylvania at
    the approximate time that Thomas Iiams resided there. There is a "Marcy
    Walton" born September 3, 1807 died August 25, 1866 who married a James Hughes.
    Her older sister Phoebe died April 1, 1827 in her 22nd year. This would make
    Phoebe being born about 1800-1805 and Marcy would have been born after this
    date about 1807. This would be too late for this Mercy to have married
    Thomas Iiams. In the 'Keyhole' The Genealogical Society of Southwestern
    Pennsylvania Vol. XXVI No. 3 July 1998, page 112, it states that Mercy Walton, born
    1800 died young, she was the daughter of Jonathan Walton and Kesiah Rebecca
    Moore Walton. [The source: The Ten Mile Country by Howard L. Lecky, page 66 and
    Rural Reflections of Amwell Township, Washington County, Pa. Vol. III, page
    95].
    The following entry is the partition of land owned by Aaron Bonnell,
    deceased and the relationship of his daughter Mercy.
    Washington County, Pennsylvania Partitions 1781-1850 [page 33]
    Bonnell, Aaron West
    Bethlehem township
    "died March 1813" Dec term
    1831 #27
    Wife: Ann
    Ch: Elizabeth Schooley; Moses, dec'd - his children [Lewis, Ann, Jane,
    Aaron, William, Henry; Mary, w/o James Buckingham; Sarah, w/o Thomas Hutton;
    Anna, w/o Edward Wallace - their children, Mary, Ann, Richard, William,
    Thomas, Edward and John, and one not named; Marcy, w/o Thomas Iiams, Shelby County,
    Ohio. March 23, 1842; Benjamin; John; Eleanor, w/o Thomas Moore, their
    children, Ann, Eunice, Thomas, Esther, William Narcissa, Aaron; David and William.
    This partition would then prove that Marcy is the daughter of Aaron
    Bonnell and the wife of Thomas Iiams.
    As we all know, once in print, it is difficult to prove that the entry could
    be wrong. Lecky did a great service to us all in his genealogy research. I
    am thankful for that.
