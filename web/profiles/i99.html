    The following narrative from Roberta Iiames, Springboro, Ohio was sent to me March 24, 2003 and reads as follows:
    "While his two older brothers left the area, Samuel, William and James stayed in Ohio. He was a farmer, a cabinet-maker and a carpenter. About 1885, he removed to Lima, Allen County, Ohio from Shelby County, Ohio. He worked for the C. H. & D. shops in Lima, Ohio and lived at 1136 E. Elm Street. His land transactions in Shelby County, Ohio were numerous. He bought land and sold other parcels of land to Mary's father, David Abbott."

    "Samuel was a volunteer of the 19th Regiment "G" of V.O.I. of the War of the Rebellion. He entered the service on September 26, 1864 and was discharged June 8, 1865. From his pension records, it is evident that he had to fight for his pension. We copied a paper from the Family Bible indicating he was assigned an attorney in Washington, D. C. His name was recorded as "Samuel James" not "Samuel Iiames." On his military papers, he is described as being 5'7" in height, gray eyes and dark hair and fair complexion."

    "His death notice in the Lima newspaper reads: "...Death came Thursday evening at 7:20 caused from heart trouble and took from our vicinity a devoted father, a thoughtful neighbor and friend with a patience which none but God can give..." He had been a resident of Lima for 32 years at the time of his death; he was a carpenter at the C. H. & D. shops until about 5 years ago. He was a member of the U. B. Church and he has always been a man of fine qualities. "Although his death falls like a blight on our lives his heart will know no sadness; although he will be sadly missed we know that he is peacefully at rest where he will no longer know no pain." He leaves to mourn, Harvey Iiames, Ms. H. C. Disman, James Iiames, Thomas Iiames of Cincinnati, Frank Iiams with the American E. F. in France and William Iiames, Lima and 18 grandchildren and four great-grandchildren."
