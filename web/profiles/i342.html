    Biography [2050]
    Isaac Laubach was the ninth of ten children. Adam and Elizabeth Berger Laubach, his parents, had nine sons. He was baptized on April 27, 1806 in Williams Twp. in the Old Williams Congregation Church (now St. Luke's Lutheran Church). He was confirmed on November 2, 1821 in the Old Lower Saucon Twp. Church (Christ Church), in Lower Saucon Twp., PA. He was just eighteen years old when he married Elizabeth Kiefer, the daughter of Andrew and Elizabeth Kiefer. In 1830, he purchased the first building lot in Riegelsville, Bucks County, PA for $50 from Benjamin "The Miller" Riegel (1792-1860). He built a stone house there in 1834 with the inscription above the doorway I.L.1834. In 1838, he assisted with the construction of the Riegelsville Inn for another Benjamin Riegel; Benjamin "The Farmer" Riegel (1777-1848). Like his father before him, Isaac Laubach was a master stone mason. After the death of his first wife he sold the Riegelsville home for $650 to Samuel Fox from Durham Twp., Bucks County, PA. He moved his family to Finesville, Warren County, NJ. where he built another stone home along the Musconetcong River.

    The second wife of Isaac Laubach was Susanna Kiefer. She was the younger sister of his first wife. Isaac Laubach died of consumption (tuberculosis) at the age of 67. His tombstone is located near the center of the eastern wall of the Finesville Union Cemetery in Finesville, Pohatcong, Twp., Warren County, NJ.

    George Laubach, his grandfather and Adam Laubach, his father, were veterans of the American Revolution. Isaac S. Laubach, his grandson, was a Civil War veteran.

    Note #1

    Isaac Laubach was born in Wlliams Twp., Northampton County, PA. in a stone-stucco colonial two story dwelling erected by his father Adam Laubach in 1803. The house is located on Texas Road about 1/4 mile from Buttermilk Road in Williams Twp. The Adam Laubach House is located about 1/4 mile from the George Laubach House also located on Texas Road. Adam Laubach (1760-1828) was the father of Isaac Laubach.

    The George Laubach House was originally a log cabin with a stone foundation and shallow basement. The house was erected (ca.1768) and has since been built around whereby concealing the original construction. A portion of the original cabin wall can be seen from within the house. It is one of the oldest structures in Williams Twp. George Laubach (1726-1802) was the grandfather of Isaac Laubach.

    The Christian Laubach Homestead (ca. 1748) is located a few miles from the Adam and George Laubach homesteads. It is located on Hellertown Road in Lower Saucon Township, PA. Christian Laubach (1699-1768) was the great-grandfather of Isaac Laubach. Christian Laubach was trained in Germany as a soldier. He was captain of the "Saucon Rangers" during the French and Indian Wars.
    Christian Laubach emigrated with his family from Budingen, Germany and landed at Philadelphia, PA on September 16, 1738. He worked at the Durham Forge as a blacksmith. He also aquired hundreds of acres in Lower Saucon Twp. His miltia unit the "Saucon Rangers" protected what was then the American frontier in and around Nazareth, PA from Indian attacks. The Rangers under his leadership staged an early morning mission near Wind Gap after camping outside of the Rose Inn Tavern just outside of Nazareth. The Rose Inn Tavern also served as an early stockade fort. About one month after his mission in Nazareth, Captain Laubach, along with his son George and other local inhabitants, traveled with Benjamin Franklin to what is now Weissport, PA. Upon arrival, Benjamin Franklin supervised the construction of a proper frontier fort for the protection of local inhabitants against Indian raids. Fort Allen was constructed and guarded for some time by Captain Christian Laubach and several others. Fort Allen was named in honor of William Allen; Find-A-Grave #62239092. Fort Allen served as the prototype for many frontier forts which were constructed during the French and Indian Wars.
