    William Iiams or Ijams settled in Anne Arundel Co., Maryland about 1650 
    [b.before 1640]. Some kept the spelling as Iiams or Ijams but other changed the 
    name. Plummer John Ijams owned a Mill in Frederick Co., Md. and about 1831 he 
    gave the B&O RR a 'right of way' through his land. The company agreed to 
    establish a depot and the name would be "Ijams Mill" which later was changed to 
    "Ijamsville." Most of the Iams, Imes, Iiames, Iiams, Ijams & Ijames can be 
    traced back to William Iiams [Ijams] b. 1640 and who married Elizabeth 
    Cheney. 

    Thomas Lewis Iames or Imes b. Dec. 4, 1866 Bedford Co., Pa. d. March 9, 
    1934, s/o Davie Imes and Emma Ann Leasure. He married first to Emma Elizabeth 
    Cooper b. June 26, 1873 and d. June 18, 1920. He married second Matilda 
    Northcraft who was b. 1877 d. 1943.
    I show 7 children of the first marriage.

    Barbara Iiams b. May 2, 1836 in Clearville, Bedford Co., Pa. d. March 29, 
    1879 in Clearville, Pa. Married William Redinger January 5, 1853. I show only 
    one daughter, Mary Ann Redinger who married a John Calvin Amick. Barbara 
    Iiams or Imes was the daughter of Amos Hiram Iiams b. abt 1783 in Maryland and d. 
    About 1851. He was the s/o of William Iiams and Elizabeth. There were 8 
    children. After the death of Elizabeth, Amos Hiram Iiams married Harriet Beall 
    and there were no children of this union. Do you know the parents of William 
    Redinger, if so, could you send the names to me? Evidently, from your post 
    and my information we don't exactly agree. Open for discussion.
