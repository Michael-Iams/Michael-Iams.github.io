    Obituary
    Frank C Iams, 71, died at his home Thursday, September 5, 1991.

    He was born May 18, 1920, at Dwight, the son of Corb and Iva Ruth Seley Iams. He spent his early life in Dwight, Harveyville and Chalk communities. He farmed before serving in the Army during World War II. He lived in Topeka 30 years before moving to Osage City in 1979.

    Mr Iams was a switchman 34 years for Santa Fe Railway and was a switch foreman when he retired in 1980.

    Mr Iams was a 30-year member of United Transportation Union, a member of Capital Post No 1 of the American Legion, Topeka Lodge No 17, AF & AM, and a 25-year member of Fraternal Order of Eagles Aerie No 58, all in Topeka. He also was a member of Osage City Lions Club abd Osage City Country Club. He was a former member of University United Methodist Church in Topeka.

    He was married to Etta Lorraine Arnett. She died in 1958. He was married to Juanita Tice on June 17, 1960, at LAs Vegas, Nevada. She survives.

    Other survivors include a daughter, Vicki Berberich, Osage City; three sons, Frank Eugene Iams, Overland Park, Gary H Iams and Vincent L Alexander, both of Topeka; his mother, Iva Ruth Iams, Janesville, Minnesota; a sister, Marge Cowdin, Janesville, Minnesota; three brothers, Heorge Iams, Longmont, Colorado, Herbert Iams, Glendo, Wyoming, and Lawrence Iams, Ls Vegas, Nevada; eight grandchildren; and five great-grandchildren.

    Memorial contributions may be made to Kansas Masonic Home.

    Services were at 10 am Monday, September 9, 1991 at Crable Funeral Chapel in Osage City, Kansas with The Rev Robert Iams officiating. Masonic Graveside Services by Topeka Lodge No 17, AF & AM with interment following at the Topeka Cemetery in Topeka, Kansas. Arrangements were provided by Crable Funeral Chapel in Osage City, Kansas.
