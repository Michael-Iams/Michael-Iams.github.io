Alexander Murdoch IamsObituaryTranscribed by Laura Nelle Poland Mallett on February 26, 2006. [72]
Dr. Alexander M. Iams, 66, of Madison, Wis., died Monday, February 13, 1984, as the result of a massive coronary.
Dr. Iams was born April 29, 1917, in Pittsburgh, a son of Dr. J. Donald and Grace Donnan Iams. The family moved to Washington in August 1931.
He was graduated from East Washington High School, class of 1934, Washington and Jefferson College in 1938 and the University of Pennsylvania Medical School in 1942. He interned at Mercy Hospital in Pittsburgh and in July 1943 was accepted as a Fellow in pediatrics at Mayo Clinic in Rochester, Minn. He completed his fellowship in pediatrics in late 1946 and in Jan. 1947 began practicing in Minneapolis. In 1948, he set up practice in Madison, Wis., where he practiced until his death.
He was affiliated with Madison General Hospital and the University of Wisconsin Hospital and taught some classes at the University of Wisconsin Medical School.
Surviving are his wife, Virginia Busbey Iams; two sons, Dr. Jay D. Iams of Columbus, Ohio, and William Mitchell Iams, Madison, Wis.; and one daughter, Mary Virginia, wife of John M. Daniels, of Brownsville, Texas; one sister, Charlotte D. Iams of Washington; one brother, Franklin P. Iams of Falls Church, Va.; five grandchildren, Andrew Jay Iams, Steven Edward Iams, Alexander Guilfoyle Iams, Mary Patricia Iams and Sara Virginia Iams.

Hannah Margaret HoskinsonObituaryTranscribed by Laura Nelle Poland Mallett on March 14, 2006 [72]
Mrs. Hannah M. Iams, wife of W. L. Iams, died at her home on East College Street, this place, Sabbath evening, April 21, 1895, ten minutes to six o'clock.
She had suffered for several years with asthma. On the Monday night previous to her faith, she took a heavy cold, as they believed, but it developed into congestion of the lungs together with la grippe. It was a severe shock to the family and friends, as she appeared better Sabbath morning. She bore her suffering patiently, and was always bright and cheerful. She lived to make the world better for her having lived in it. She was a member of the Cumberland Presbyterian Church, and a very earnest and devoted Christian. In the Sunday School, and in the Woman's Christian Temperance Union, she was always willing to perform her part of the work. On the Sabbath evening before she died, she took part in the Easter services, having drilled a class of little girls. A friend speaking to her about it during her sickness remarked: "Mrs. Iams, I fear you worked too hard and exposed yourself preparing for the Easter services." Her reply was:"Somebody had to do it." She never said:" I can't, but , "I will try," was her motto when asked to work for her Master.

The church has lost an earnest worker, the Sabbath School a faithful teacher, the husband a loving, cheerful and industrious wife, and the daughter a noble mother. While she felt if she could be spared a few years longer she could accomplish more for humanity, yet if it was the Lord's will, she was willing to obey the call. It was her dying request that they sing and pray. As the friends were singing "Nearer My God To Thee" she made a feeble attempt to join with them, but she was too weak to utter an audible sound. The three most beautiful words seemed linked together-ful - "Mother, Home, and Heaven" She seemed to realize the words of the hymn, and was drawing nearer and nearer. And as He was passing by she reached out her hand and touched the border of His garmet, "and the woman was made whole from that hour," She closed her eyes in sleep-"Asleep in Jesus, Blessed Sleep, From Which None Ever Wake to Weep, From Which None Ever Wake to Weep."
The Memorial Services were conducted by Rev. A. B. Miller, DD, of the C. P. Church, after which the remains were borne to Greene Mount cemetery, where they were laid to rest. The husband and daughter have the sympathy of a large circle of friends, as was manifested by their presence as a token of respect.

Franklin Pierce IamsObituaryTranscribed by Laura Nelle Poland Mallett on March 10, 2006 [72]
Franklin P. Iams, 71, died Nov. 18, 1986, in Sarasota General Hospital, Sarasota, Florida, of cancer.
Born in Pittsburgh, the son of the late Dr. J. Donald Iams and Grace Donnan Iams, he moved to Washington following the death of his father.
He graduated from East Washington High School in 1933 and from Washington and Jefferson College in 1937, where he received a bachelor of arts degree in business administration.
After serving in the United States Army from 1941 to 1946, Mr. Iams received his masters of arts degree in hospital administration from the University of Minnesota in 1948.

Mr. Iams served on President Lyndon B. Johnson's committee on US Health Service in 1965 and the following year served on the President's committee on emergency preparedness programs.
