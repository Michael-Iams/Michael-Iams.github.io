Badgers young, old mark 100th
by Heidi Rowley on Sep 11, 2006, under Local, Tucson Citizen
http://tucsoncitizen.com/morgue/2006/09/11/25799-badgers-young-old-mark-100th/

It’s been a while since Charles Farrell graduated from Tucson High School. How about 78 years ago?
Farrell is the only living graduate from the class of 1928 and was honored Sunday night during the school’s centennial celebration as the most senior living THS graduate.
About 2,000 people attended the event involving entertainment for the alumni, current students and future students.
Farrell sat in the auditorium with his family, listening to the band play.
Pointing to the stage, the 96-year-old said, “The first solo I ever sang was up in here.”
Farrell was part of the Boys Glee Club and said his first song was “Russian Lullaby.”
Holly Bolen, 11, attended the event with her grandfather, Rudy Castro, a 1949 graduate.
Bolen will graduate in 2012, her grandmother, Caroline Castro, said. She comes with a lot of Tucson High history.
Rudy Castro’s mother was a 1925 graduate and his wife worked at the school for five years as a campus monitor.
“All of us have a touch of Badger in us,” Caroline Castro said, referring to the school’s mascot.
Outside the auditorium, current students ran fundraising booths for their various clubs, selling water, soda, carne asada, egg rolls, slushies and other things to eat.
Friends Nikki Tomastis, 15, and Erica Bacher, 16, are on the school’s dance team, which had one of the booths.
Their group performed outside later in the evening.
After talking about all the alumni who showed up for the event, Bacher said with enthusiasm, “When I’m 116, I’ll be here for the (200th) celebration.”
Former football players and graduates of the class of 1945 Armando Carrillo, 80, and Bob Canfield, 78, know what it’s like to visit the school again with their friends. They embraced on the steps of Tucson High.
“It’s not a surprise the school is still here. It will go on forever. Tucson High is the beginning of everything,” said Canfield.
Dorothy Elliotte Ijams, who will be 90 Tuesday, attended the celebration, escorted by her son, Kirt Ijams.
She graduated in 1933 and said she gets together with a group of graduates named the Golden Oldies who do dinners together.
Ijams said she is the youngest of the group. As she had just arrived, Ijams wasn’t sure if other members of her group attended the celebration.

ObituaryOct 25, 2016The Arizona Daily Star, Tucson, Pima, Arizona, United States
IJAMS, Dorothy Berenice (Elliotte) 100, died on October 15, 2016.

A 2nd generation Tucsonan, Dorothy lived in Armory Park, attended Safford School and was a 1933 graduate of Tucson Senior High School. Following High School, Dorothy began a four decade career as a cosmetology instructor.

Dorothy married Harold Ijams in 1938 and lived for six decades in Tucson's Palo Verde neighborhood.

Dorothy was predeceased by her mother, Dorris Elliotte; brother, Harold Elliotte; sister, Edythe Stubler, Edythe's two children and Dorothy's husband Harold Ijams.

She is survived by her sons, Donald Ijams (Kenith Wertman Ijams) and Kirt Ijams (Nancy Montoya-Ijams); grandchildren, Amy (Mark Joyner), Ben, Matt (Michelle) and Hal; great-grandchildren, Jake, Isabelle, Aria, Sawyer and extended family including James Ijams (Maxine), Carl Phillip Ijams, Era Ijams and many nephews, nieces and their children.

At the time of her death, Dorothy was living at the Villas at La Canada where she received much loving care and friendship. She expired peacefully on the morning of October 15, 2016.

Private services are pending.

Arrangements ADAIR FUNERAL HOME, Dodge Chapel.

Dorothy's sister:

by Tucson Citizen on Apr 08, 2000, 
EDYTHE L. ”SHEILAH” STUBLER
died March 31, 2000 after losing her battle with cancer. Born April 6, 1925, she was a lifelong resident of Tucson. She was preceded in death by her children, Barbara Elizabeth Stubler and Michael Joseph Stubler. She is survived by her grandchildren, Christopher Stubler and Devon Stubler, and her long time friends, Rudi Maria Dogoli-Beals and James Beals. Also surviving are her sister, Dorothy (Harold) Ijams and several nephews and nieces, including Donald (Kay) Ijams, Kirt (Nancy) Ijams and Sabra Jeeves, and numerous great nieces and nephews.
Edythe had a long and successful career as a supervisor at U.S. West and AT&T in Tucson. She was a member of the Tucson Life Members Club of the Telephone Pioneers. She volunteered at St. Joseph’s Hospital Auxiliary and El Dorado Hospitals where she helped many and made numerous friends. For years, she volunteered for the Salvation Army to help feed and serve the homeless. Her family and many friends, including those at the Crop Shop and at Canyon Crest, will miss her greatly. According to her wishes, no services are planned. Arrangements entrusted to HEATHER MORTUARY.
