Dale CoonyObituaryApr 7, 1942Lima News, McGuffey, Hardin, Ohio, United States
Dale H. Cooney

McGUFFEY, April 7 - Funeral services for Dale H. Cooney, 31, will be held at 2:30 p.m. Wednesday at the late home north of McGuffey, with Rev. J. M. Gambill, pastor of the Huntersville Methodist church in charge.

Cooney died in Kenton McKitrick hospital of injuries received early Sunday morning when the motorcycle he was riding apparently left the road on a curve and struck a pole. His death was caused by a skull fracture. The mishap occurred about two and a half miles east of the Route 69 intersection near the Huntersville grange hall. He was found at 7 a.m. by Russell Ream, of Ada, who took him to the Kenton hospital. It is believed that the accident occurred at about 6 a.m.

Cooney is survived by his parents, Mr. and Mrs. Spencer H. Cooney; his wife, Nellie; a daughter, Connie; a niece, Mary Ann Keller, and several aunts and uncles. Two sisters were killed in an automobile accident several years ago.

(published in The Lima News, Tuesday, April 7, 1942)

Mary DaltorioObituaryDec 13, 1977McGuffey, Hardin, Ohio, United States
Mary Iams

McGUFFEY - Mary Iams, 60, died at 8:20 a.m. Monday in Riverside Hospital, Columbus, following a lengthy illness.

Born Nov. 21, 1917, in Akron, she was a daughter of Edward and Julia A. Kopaz Daltorio. She married Roy Iams on April 10, 1941, and he survives.

Other survivors include a daughter, Mrs. John (Beth) Culbertson of Troy; a son, James of Union, S.C.; two grandchildren; and four brothers, Raymond Daltorio of Cincinnati, Phillip Daltorio of Gallipolis, Albert Daltorio of Peninsula and Alfred Daltorio of California.

A member of the McGuffey United Methodist Church, she taught business and English courses at Kenton High School.

Services will be at 11 a.m. Thursday in Hanson Funeral Home, Ada, Rev. James O. Lewis officiating. Burial will be in Woodlawn Cemetery, Ada.

Friends may call at the funeral home from 2 to 4 and 7 to 9 p.m. Wednesday. Memorials may be made to the Hardin County unit of the American Cancer Society.

(published in The Lima News, Tuesday, December 13, 1977)
