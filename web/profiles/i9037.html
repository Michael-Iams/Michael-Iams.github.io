    ObituaryFeb 17, 1891Columbus Daily Enquirer, Columbus, Muscogee, Georgia, United States
    Dr. DeVOTIE DEAD
    He Passes Away at 7 O'clock Yesterday Morning – To be Buried Here

    Dr. J.H. DeVotie is dead. The good man breathed his last at 7 o'clock yesterday morning, at his home in Griffin.

    The sad news of his death reached Columbus a few hours afterwards, through a telegram to Dr. Estes.

    The telegram was from Mrs. DeVotie, instructing that a grave be dug in the family lot here, and preparations be made for the funeral. The body will reach Columbus at 11:25 o'clock this morning and will be taken at once to the cemetery.

    Dr. DeVotie had been confined to his bed for about three months and for some time prior to his death it was known that the end was near. Dr. DeVotie was well known in Columbus and to the entire Baptist denomination throughout the country. To him Columbus owes much for his able and serious work in securing for her one of the best public school systems in the country. There are many college and churches in this country [with] monuments [dedicated] to his memory.

    Dr. James Harvey D'Votie.
    James Harvey DeVotie, D.D., was born in Oneida county, N.Y., September 24, 1813. He was baptized Sunday, December 4, 1831, at Savannah, Ga., by Rev. H.O. Wyer. The First Baptist church of Savannah licensed him to preach the gospel on the 21st of October, 1832. He was ordained at Camden, S.C., in 1838, by Jesse Hartwell, D.D., and Joseph E. Cook, D.D. He served his first church at Camden, and afterwards served churches in Montgomery, Ala., Marion, Ala., and Siloam, Ala. He was at this time made financial secretary of the Domestic Missions Board of the Southern Baptist Convention.

    The older Baptists of Alabama will remember how hard Dr. DeVotie worked to raise an endowment fund for Howard college, which is now at Birmingham. He traveled the entire State, mostly on horseback, to raise this large amount, but succeeded, as was his determination in all things he undertook.

    In 1856 he was called by the First Baptist church of Columbus, which church he served for fourteen years, and the good people of this city will remember his many good works for ages to come. It is here his family are buried.

    In 1870 Dr DeVotie moved from Columbus to Griffin and took charge of a church. He has lived in Griffin since his removal from Columbus.

    He was made corresponding secretary of the Mission Board of the State convention in 1876 or 1877, which position he held at the time of his death.

    Dr. DeVotie was the father of fifteen children, only one of whom is living, Miss Georgia DeVotie, now a student at the Southern Female College at LaGrange.

    In the death of Dr. DeVotie, the State has lost one of its best citizens and the Baptist denomination one of its most devoted and eminent workers.

    [Source Finda-a-Grave citing: Tuesday, February 17, 1891; Columbus Daily Enquirer (Columbus, GA); Volume: XXXIII /Issue: 43 /Page: 1]
