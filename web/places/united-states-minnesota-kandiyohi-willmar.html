Robert Wayne IiamsObituaryWest Central Tribune, Willmar, Kandiyohi, Minnesota, United States
Robert Iiams Oct. 27, 1929 - May 22, 2011

KANDIYOHI -- Robert Wayne Iiams, 81, of Kandiyohi died Sunday at Bethesda Pleasant View in Willmar.
The funeral service will be at 11 a.m. Thursday at Harvey Anderson Funeral Home in Willmar. Burial will be in Union Cemetery in Atwater.
Visitation will be from 5 to 7 p.m. Wednesday at the funeral home and one hour prior to the service on Thursday.
Robert Wayne Iiams was born on Oct. 27, 1929, in Montezuma, Iowa, the son of George and Beulah (Abel) Iiams. He grew up in Iowa, graduated from Tingly High School in the class of 1945 and attended Iowa State College in Iowa City, receiving an associate degree in 1947. Bob entered the United States Navy in 1948. He served in the Korean War and later in Vietnam.
He was united in marriage to Lela Whitlow on Feb. 6, 1954, in Portsmouth, Va. He continued his service in the Navy, serving as a senior chief petty officer. He received an honorable discharge in 1976 and retired from the Navy. Also, during this time, Bob purchased a gas station in Portsmouth, which he operated until 1969, when they moved to Minnesota and he purchased the Texaco gas station in Spicer. Bob and Lela lived in Spicer until they purchased a farm in Kandiyohi where they owned and operated Iiams Garage.
Bob attended Grace Baptist Church in Willmar where he served as an usher. He was a member of the American Party, the Republican Party, American Legion Post 167, and Retired Servicemen's Club.
Bob enjoyed basketball, bowling, golf, playing cards, having coffee and visiting with friends. He also enjoyed music, Lawrence Welk and Fun Time Polka. He was always interested in current events, following the news and politics. Bob cherished the times he could spend with his family, especially his grandchildren.
Bob is survived by his wife, Lela, of Kandiyohi; sons, Robert Iiams Jr. of Minneapolis, Joey (and Glennyce) Iiams and grandchildren, Ethan Iiams and Megan Iiams, all of Kandiyohi. He is also survived by his sisters, Beverly (and Jim) Calvert of Marietta, Ohio, and Linda (Terry) Waychoff of Watkins, Iowa.
He was preceded in death by his parents; sister, Betty McGuire; and a sister in infancy.
Lela Lerline WhitlowObituaryWest Central Tribune, Willmar, Kandiyohi, Minnesota, United States
Lela L. Iiams, 83, of rural Kandiyohi, died Monday, February 6th at her home. A private graveside service will be at Union Cemetery in Atwater. Memorials are preferred. Arrangements are with Harvey Anderson Funeral Home in Willmar. www.hafh.org
Lela Lerline Iiams was born on December 3, 1933, to Aubrey and Alice (Ratcliff) Whitlow in Roanoke, VA. She was one of five children. Lela attended her schooling in Roanoke. She later moved to Portsmouth where she met Robert Iiams at a roller rink. On February 6, 1954, Lela and Robert married at Geneva Park of Christ. To this union two sons were born. After marriage, Lela and Robert lived in Portsmouth, VA, until Robert retired from the service in 1969. After retirement they moved to rural Kandiyohi, where they spent time on their hobby farm. Lela worked at Langer's clothing store in the Kandi Mall from 1987 to 1994. She took great joy in being a mom and especially a grandma. She enjoyed gardening and teaching her grandchildren different gardening tricks. On May 22, 2011, Lela lost Robert, he was the love of her life.
Lela is survived by her sons: Robert Iiams of San Diego, CA, and Joey (Glenneyce) Iiams of Kandiyohi; grandchildren: Ethan Iiams and Megan Iiams; special friend, Sam Lungstrom, and siblings: AJ (Paula) Whitlow, Eugene (Nancy) Whitlow, Carlton (Peggy) Whitlow, and Donnie (Bonita) Whitlow.
She was preceded in death by her husband, Robert, and her parents.
