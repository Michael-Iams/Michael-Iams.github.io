38-55 - IJAMS, DORSEY, OWINGS, HIGGINS - Mar 1824
Thomas Beall OWINGS and wife Cordelia, James L. HIGGINS and wife Harriet, administrators of Nathan HARRIS, deceased vs John IJAMS Jr Harriet was previously the widow of Basil DORSEY when she and Thomas Beall OWINGS and Cordelia became administrators of Nathan HARRIS' Estate. John IJAMS Jr was indebted to Nathan HARRIS. On 2 Nov 1822, John IJAMS conveyed to James L. HIGGINS (not then married to Harriet) and William DOWNEY 180 acres on Bush Creek where Luke DAVIS was then residing (for no monies) a Deed of Trust for the use of John IJAMS Jr.and his wife Harriet H. IJAMS during their natural lives and after their deaths, to descend to their lawful heirs, should they have any; if not, to descend to Joseph H. IJAMS and his heirs; if he dies without heirs, then to descend to the nephews and nieces of the said John IJAMS and their heirs. On 19 May 1823, John IJAMS Jr. (then living in Fairfield Co, Ohio) executed to Thomas ANDERSON of Frederick County, MD a Deed of Conveyance, which recited the previous Deed of Trust plus 1/2 of additional lands. By now, both Harriet H. IJAMS and Joseph H. IJAMS are also believed to be living in Ohio. On 17 Sep 1825, sale was held and high bidder was Thomas B. OWINGS for $230.

Rebecca IiamsObituaryOct 14, 1863The Examiner, Frederick, Maryland, United States
14 Oct 1863 - The Examiner, Frederick, MD

Near Frederick City on the morning of the 12th, instant, very suddenly from disease of the heart, REBECCA WILCOXON, consort of John Wilcoxon, Esq. age 47 years, 1 month and 2 days. It is solemn at any time to record the death of a friend. But when we are called upon to place among the annals of death one so noble and pure as this subject, it is imminently sure to cause grief of the most obdurate character. Her generous heart was ever open to the poor and her hand was ever extended to the afflected. It will not only be her friends and family to morn their sudden loss, but ? they who will cherish her kind and generous heart. Her hand was ever resting in unison with her feeling soul and the grandest impulse of nature seemed to cluster over her, developing that noble and generous heart. Her memory will never be forgotten, that buoyant spirit which she wielded while on earth, will ever rest in the minds of those who knew her. Abt the fond recollection will cluster around them as the deep and heavy sighs will flow in profusion. It will ever inspire new life in the breasts of those who knew her, and when her mortal frame will have perished, beneath the solemn mounds wherein she rests, her name will live in their hearts never to be forgotten while life shall last. Her name was ever exalted while she remained with us on earth and it will be immortalized in the realms beyond the grave. She now has gone, and her kind and tender voice will never fall upon the ear of those whose delight it was to listen. The death of this generous lady was very sudden. She retired as usual, on the evening previous to her death and being taken with that dreadful disease of the heart which which she had been suffering, without a word her spirit passed away to it's Maker, as calm and serene, as the dew before the morning sun. She leaves a large family and a concourse of friends and relatives to morn her, sudden and unexpected departure from this wicked world to the one from which no traveler returns. W.T.S.

Examiner, Frederick, MD, 2 Feb 1883 - at his residence in this city, JOHN WILCOXON, aged 75 years, 1 month and 6 days.

Examiner, Frederick, MD, 7 Feb 1883 - Death of an Old Citizen -Mr. John Wilcoxon, who had been in ill-health for some time, died at his residence on All Saints street, at an early hour on Friday morning last. He was born in this city, on the 27th of December, 1807, and was consequently in the 76th year of his age. Mr. Wilcoxon was twice married, his first wife was a Miss Ijams and his second a sister of C.E. Mealey. Nine children survive him. During his long and active life he amassed a handsome competency. His funeral took place on Sunday. Interment at Mt. Olivet Cemetery. Revds. Dr. E. R. Eschbach, I. P. McCurdy and Bielfield officiating.
