    Obituary
    Richard L. Imes, 79, of Victoria Avenue, Everett, passed away on Thursday, November 28, 2013 at Homewood Retirement Center, Martinsburg, PA. He was born on May 28, 1934 in Clearville, Pennsylvania; a son of the late Perry R. and Effie (Clingerman) Imes. On January 13, 1959 in Winchester, Virginia; he married Mereta M. (Grimes) Imes. 
    He and his wife celebrated fifty-four years of marriage. 

    Along with his wife, he is survived by the following family:
    Two Children:
    Vicki L. married to Lance Sleighter, Everett, PA
    Steven R. Imes married to Tina (Uhland), Everett, PA

    Four Grandchildren:
    L. Ryan Sleighter, Michael Sleighter married to Terra, Danielle (Imes) married to Jesse Foor and Garrett Imes and fiancé Lara Haney.

    Three Great Grandchildren:
    Addison, Conner and Cyrus

    Two sisters:
    Mary Lou Baker, Wiley Ford, WV
    Genevieve married to Robert Beegle, Everett, PA

    He was preceded in death by a sister:
    Lyda Dibert

    Mr. Imes was affiliated with Zion Lutheran Church, Clearville.

    Richard served in the United States Army and was honorably discharged in 1957.

    He was employed by the Pennsylvania Turnpike Commission for twenty–eight years until his retirement in 1995, he worked as an equipment operator until his final few years he then worked on tunnel maintenance. Prior to his years of employment for the Turnpike Commission he was employed for Pennsylvania Forest and Water.

    Richard enjoyed local racing, Nascar racing, keeping his automobiles meticulous and most of all spending time with his family.

    Family and Friends will be received on Saturday, November 30, 2013 at Akers Funeral Home, 299 Raystown Road, Everett from 2-4 P.M. and 6-8 P.M. and on Sunday from 1:00 P.M. until the hour of service at the funeral home.

    Funeral Service will be held on Sunday, December 1, 2013 at 2:00 P.M. in the Chapel at Akers Funeral Home with Pastor Janet Sell officiating.

    Burial will be held in privately in Zion Lutheran Cemetery, Clearville.

    Online condolences may be expressed at www.akersfuneralhome.com
