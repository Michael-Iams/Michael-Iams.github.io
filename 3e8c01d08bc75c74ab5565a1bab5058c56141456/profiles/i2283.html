    ObituaryTranscribed by Laura Nelle Poland Mallett on March 06, 2006
    Graham P. Cowieson, 54, of 395 East Maiden Street, a funeral director in the Washington area for 26 years, died Sunday morning, July 26, 1970, in Washington Hospital, where he had been a patient the last week. He had operated a funeral home in the same location on East Maiden Street the last 24 years.
    Mr. Cowieson was born Nov. 20, 1915, in Wellsville, Ohio, a son of William R. And Gail Iams Cowieson. He was a member of the First Presbyterian Church and Washington.
    Mr. Cowieson was a past exalted ruler of BPOE Lodge No. 776, of Washington; past southwest president of the State Elks Association; past president of the Washington Kiwanis Club; a member of the Arms Club and a life member of the South Strabane Volunteer Fire Department. He was also active in many other community organizations.
    He was a member of Sunset Lodge No. 623, F&AM; past high priest of Washington Chapter No. 150; a member of Syria Temple a Pittsburgh, the Ancient Accepted Scottish Rite and Pittsburgh Consistory.
    He was married Aug. 18, 1969, to Marion Powell, of Vandergrift, who survives.
    Also surviving are the following children; C. Graham Cowieson and J. Douglas Cowieson, both of Washington; Leslie Ann, Laurie Louise and Lance Charles, all at home; two grandchildren, Carla and Cathi Cowieson; one brother, William in California, and one sister, Betty Page, of New York.
    More:
    Friends of Graham P. Cowieson, of 395 East Malden Street, who died Sunday, July 26, 1970, will be received at the Graham P. Cowieson Funeral Home from 7-9 PM Monday and 2-4 and 7-9 PM Tuesday. Services will be held there Wednesday, July 29, at 10:30 AM in charge of Dr. Joseph R. Hookey. Burial in Greene Mount Cemetery, Waynesburg. The family requests donations to be made in Mr. Cowieson's memory to the Cerebral Palsy Fund.
