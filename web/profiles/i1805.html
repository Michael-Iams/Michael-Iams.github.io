    Note #1

    Died of smallpox. Their children migrated to Ohio then some went west.

    In 1778, William, the spouse of Mary Hampton, took the Oath of Fidelity to the State of Maryland. In 1784, he moved to Washington Co. PA, and patented "Brushy Ridge", 250 acres on the north branch of Ten Mile Creek. This tract, he later conveyed to his father-in-law, Thomas Gill Hampton, who also settled in this section. William erected the first linseed oil mill in that part of the country. William's will was proved in Washington Co. PA, his brother Thomas being executor to sell his property. William was survived by Mary, his wife, and his children, Thomas, Elizabeth, John and Mary. William died from smallpox.
