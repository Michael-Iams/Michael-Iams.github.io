Elsie Leona White Iams, 87, of West Bethlehem Township, died Saturday, Jan. 16, 1993.
She was born March 18, 1905, near Clarksville, the oldest child of Omar David and Mary Piper White.
She was a longtime resident of West Bethlehem Township and Trinity High School teacher. She also taught Sunday school at North Ten Mile Baptist Church.
After graduating from West Bethlehem Township High School in 1922, she obtained her teaching certificate from California Normal School, now California University of Pennsylvania. While teaching in the East McKeesport School District, she obtained a bachelor of arts degree from the University of Pittsburgh.
In 1935, she married Byard Owen Iams. She then devoted her attention to her family and activities with North Ten Mile Baptist Church, including serving as Sunday school treasurer, youth choir director, church clerk and in other positions. Her teaching tenure at the church spanned more than 50 years.
After the death of her husband she returned to teaching at Trinity High School from 1959 until 1970, when she retired as head of the mathematics department.
She was active for many years in the Washington County Tuberculosis and Health Association, the Retired Teachers Association and the Ten Mile Baptist Association.
Until recently, she continued to teach Sunday school and perform other duties for the church.
Surviving are one son, William O. Iams of Inver Grove Heights, Minn.; two daughters, Mary I. Dobich of Bridgewater, New Jersey, and Ruth E. Fell of St. Charles, Mo.; five grandchildren, Sean Dobich and Beth, Robert, Bonnie and Rebekah Fell; four sisters, Pearl Donahoo and Iva Earnest, both of Marianna RD 1, Anna Mae Eavans of Oak Hill, Ohio, and Lois Koci of Canonsburg; and two brothers, Paul White of Marianna and John White of Centerville.
Deceased, in addition to her husband, are three sisters, Eva White, Hazel McCullough and Nell Earnest.
