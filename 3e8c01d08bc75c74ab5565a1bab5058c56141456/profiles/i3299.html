    PAWASHIN-L Archives
    From: Guyrossjr@aol.com
    Subject: Demas Iams not Demas Garber Iams
    Date: Fri, 18 Jun 2004 12:01:41 EDT

    Isaac Iams b. 1809, Elizabeth b. 1812, Elinor b. 1836, George b.
    1838, Rebecca b. 1840, Isaac b. 1842 & Sarah b. 1843, are living in Monroe County,
    Stark Twp, Ohio in 1850. In the same household is Demas Iams b. 1825. All
    born in Pennsylvania. Elizabeth Williams was born in Washington Co., Pa. Isaac
    Iams and Elizabeth Williams were married in 1832. I doubt that Demas b. 1825
    is Isaac's son most likely his brother. In the will of Isaac, it shows that
    Demas & Richard Iams of Stock Co., were debtors. I believe that Demas is the
    youngest son of Rezin Iams and Phoebe Clark as Isaac is also a son. Rezin &
    Phoebe Clark Iams also had a son Richard.
    Isaac & Demas both were hard to find in the 1850 census for Monroe
    Co., Ohio as the surname was being spelled 'James'. Getting a copy of the actual
    census it clearly shows "Iams." Part of Monroe Co., Ohio was transferred to
    Noble Co., Ohio in 1851, which made it even more difficult to locate these
    two. I had to go back to Monroe Co., Ohio to locate them. Isaac died in 1852 in
    Noble Co., Ohio. Demas married an Elizabeth Pipes or Piper, the last letter on
    their son's death certificate is difficult to read. I am ordering the Civil
    War records for Demas Imes [Iams]. I know that his wife received a pension.
    My efforts to find Demas' family started with a Richard J. Iams that I
    could not place. He was born in Ohio. He was living in Missouri then married
    in Sangamon Co., Illinois to Ellen Howlett. Before marrying, he was living in
    the household of a Nathan Plummer who was born in Ohio. When I found Demas &
    his family in Noble Co., Ohio in the 1860 census, there was Richard. Once I
    have the Civil War records, I should be able to tell just when Demas died.
    Since the 1840 census only listed "Head of Household", it is difficult
    to be completely accurate in finding the children of the "Head of Household."
    By 1850, Demas & Isaac both were in Ohio. By going through the Nathan Plummer
    and the Howlett in Sangamon Co., Illinois, I was able to find Richard, then
    Demas then Isaac.
    Does anyone else on this know anything about Demas [Dennis] Iams? The
    only reference that I have found is in Iams of America by Ralph D. Reynolds,
    page 210.
    Roberta Iiames

    Subject: Demas Iams
    Date: Mon, 16 Aug 2004 19:07:07 EDT

    Just received the Civil War papers on Demas Iams. He was born in 1825 in
    Greene Co., Pa. and died in Noble Co., Ohio. He married Elizabeth Pipes who was
    born in Ohio. They had three children: [1]: Richard J. Iams b. Sept. 25, 1854
    in Ohio d. Sept. 5, 1917 Quincy, Adams Co., Illinois. He married Feb. 25, 1881
    in Sangamon Co., Illinois to Martha Ellen Howlett. [2]: Sarah O. Iams b. abt.
    1855 in Ohio and [3]: Martha Iams b. abt 1864 in Ohio.
    In the 1850 Monroe Co., Stark Twp., Ohio census, Demas is found living with
    Isaac Iams s/o of Reason [Rezin] Iams and Phoebe Clark. In his will, Isaac
    Iams shows that Demas is listed as a 'debtor'. I don't think that Demas is the
    son of this Isaac.
    In trying to find the parents of Richard J. Iams [1854], I backed into this
    by tracking him through the census. In 1880, Richard was living in the
    household of Nathan Plumer in Christian Co., Illinois. I then found who Richard J.
    Iams [1854] had married and then traced her family history. I found that Nathan
    Plumer had married into the same family as Richard J. I then went back and
    looked at the census records for Ohio - 1860 and found Demas Iams with the
    children listed. It has been a long process but one that is proving worth while.
    Before I send for the larger pension file, and more expensive, I am hoping
    that someone can verify the parents of Demas Iams as Rezin & Phoebe Clark Iams?
    Roberta Iiames
