    Obituary
    via ancestry.com

    POSTED: October 31, 2008 Save | Print | Email
    COCHRAN, Ruth Anne Iams, 90, of Doolin Road, New Martinsville, W.Va., passed away Tuesday, October 28, 2008, in New Martinsville Health Care Center.

    She was born March 24, 1918 in New Martinsville, daughter of the late Frank Victor and Mary Elizabeth Harker Iams

    Ruth Anne graduated from Magnolia High School in 1936. She attended Parkersburg Business School prior to WWII. She served as New Martinsville City Recorder in her brothers stead during the years of WWII.

    She was a member of the Limestone Methodist Church; the Trillium Garden Club; and PEO. She served as a 4-H leader; Sunday School teacher; and Literacy Volunteer.

    Ruth Anne was a Nursery Owner and Landscape Designer. She and her husband, Noel, were in the Christmas Tree Business for 40 years. She enjoyed gardening and working the soil, growing plants, flowers and trees of all kinds.

    By Ruth Annes prior arrangement and direction, her body has been donated to medical research. In time her ashes will be interred on the family farm on Doolin.

    In addition to her parents, she was preceded in death by her husband of 56 years, Noel Phillip Cochran in 2002; two brothers, John Victor Iams in 1981 and Wesley Archibold Iams in 1905; one sister, Mary Louise Iams in 1914; and an infant sibling in 1910.

    She is survived by her son, Mark Iams Cochran of New Martinsville; two granddaughters, Gena Renee Glover and Angela Dawn (Jack) McIsaac, both of Niles, Ohio; and three great-grandson, Zachary Damon Glover, Clayton Daniel McIsaac and Cole Tucker McIsaac all of Niles, Ohio.

    Friends will be received from 1 p.m. until time of memorial service at 2 p.m. led by the Rev. Donald Rush at the Limestone Methodist Church at Sunday, November 2, 2008.

    Arrangements entrusted to Campbell-Plumly-Milburn Funeral Home, 319 N. Chestnut Street, Barnesville, OH 43713.

    Memorial contributions may be made to the Evergreen Scholarship Fund, c/o Amy Cibik, 1216 Wren Drive, New Martinsville, WV 26155.

    Condolences online at www.campbellplumlymilburnfuneralhome.com
