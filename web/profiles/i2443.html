    RACHEL LEE IJAMS WED IN PRINCETON TO J. P. SCHMADER
    Published: April 13, 1986

    Rachel Lee Ijams, a daughter of Mrs. Edwards Mathews Crane Jr. of Princeton, N.J., and Fishers Island, L.I., and Porter Ijams of Locust Valley, L.I., was married yesterday to James Peter Schmader, a son of Mr. and Mrs. John Jacob Schmader Jr. of Solon, Ohio. The Rev. Frederick S. Baldwin performed the ceremony at Trinity Episcopal Church in Princeton.

    Katherine Porter Ijams was her sister's maid of honor and John Jacob Schmader 3d his brother's best man.

    Mrs. Schmader until recently was a therapist in the alcoholism program at the Counseling and Psychotherapy Center in Hamilton Square, N.J., and expects to become a senior counselor next month at Cedar House, a social service organization in Bridgewater, N.J. She graduated from the Garrison Forest School and William Smith College and received a master's degree from the Hunter College School of Social Work. Her father is retired and was formerly president of the National Quotation Bureau in New York. Her mother, Jean Drummond Crane, is a representative in Princeton of Christie's, the auction house. Her stepfather is the vice president of the Council for Financial Aid to Education in New York and chairman of Crane, Russak & Company, publishers in New York.

    Mr. Schmader, who is known as Peter, is the managing editor of Data Communications magazine, a McGraw-Hill publication in New York. He graduated from the Gilmour Academy and Villanova University. His father is the chairman and co-founder and his mother, June Cummings Schmader, is the corporate secretary and a director of the Tool-Die Engineering Company in Cleveland.
