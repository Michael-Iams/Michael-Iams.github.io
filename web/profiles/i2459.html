    Served in the Civil War as a Private, Company G, 176 Regiment O.V.I.
    He had enlisted September 16 1864 as a resident of Sarahsville, Noble Co ., OH; and was discharged at Columbus, Ohio, June 14, 1865 (or discharg ed at Nashville, TN), and moved from Columbus, Ohio, to Chicago in March 1 888, working as a carriage drive for a livery in 1902. In his pension app licated he listed his occupation as tending a hotel; was 5ft 8 1/2 in tal l, weighed 150 pounds, had blue eyes and grey hair; said he was not marri ed and that he had no children. On April 25, 1910 he gave a change of add ress to the Dept of Interior and moved to 644 MAPLE Ave in Los Angeles, C A. Widow Ella L. IJAMS filed a petition from Noble County, Ohio, Feb 1 6, 1924; and the petiton included affidavits from William J. Ijames, age 4 2, Columbus, Phio; son Thomas J. IJAMS and son John C.
