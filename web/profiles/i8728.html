Obituary
Marjorie Haney Cubbage, 86, of Charlottesville, passed away peacefully on Sunday, September 11, 2016, at the Hospice (of the Piedmont) House on Park Street in Charlottesville.She was born on April 23, 1930 in Charlottesville, Va. She was predeceased by her husband of 66 years, Lindbergh L. Cubbage. She was also preceded in death by her parents, L. F. Haney, Sr., and Anna Thomas Haney, of Barboursville, Va.; brothers, L. F. Haney, Jr., and Billy Baker Haney; and her sister, Jean Haney Thurston.

She is survived by her two sons, Michael L. Cubbage and his wife, Jan, of Keswick, Va., and Christopher L. Cubbage and his partner, Melinda Zobrist, of Greenwood, Va. She is also survived by her grandchildren, Jennifer Iams of McLean, Va., Matthew Cubbage of Atlanta, Ga., Frannie Massaro and her husband, Jim, of Rochester, N.Y., Isabelle Cubbage of Dayton, Ohio, and Nick Schaller of Dayton, Ohio; and great-grandchildren, Connor and Madeleine Iams of McLean, Va., Wyatt Massaro of Rochester, N.Y., Maison and Micah Cubbage of Atlanta, Ga.; the children of Christopher's partner, Max, Mia, Maverick, and Marlin Somers of Greenwood, Va.; a dear friend and sister-in-law, Irene Haney of Barboursville, Va.; and sisters-in-law Ann Gaylord and Katherine Goodridge and her husband, Tom, all of Charlottesville.She was surrounded by the love of many nephews and nieces. Special thanks to Kenny and Rebecca Thurston of Wintergreen, Myles Thurston of Keswick, Susan Gross of Barboursville, and Robert and LeWalter Haney of Louisa for their devotion to Margie.

She was raised on a farm in Barboursville, the current site of Barboursville Vineyards. She attended Orange County High School, where she was a star basketball and softball player. She passed along her athletic genes to both sons, and she was a huge sports fan her entire life.Margie married young and her life was driven by her faith, family and friends. She had a few part-time jobs, but her time was spent as a devoted homemaker who attended thousands of athletic events in her lifetime. A lifelong member of First United Methodist Church, she was in Women's Group 3 Circle and worked the soup kitchen for years.One of her fondest jobs was working as a caretaker of the 9 hole McIntire Golf Course, and she so enjoyed the friendships made there and all the places in Charlottesville she frequented.She was a giver and became a caregiver to both her parents and also to her husband Lindy in their later years.Special thanks to her primary care doctor for 24 years, Dr. John Schorling, her cardiologist, Dr. Jamie Kennedy, and her dentist, Dr. Steve Clarke.

She really appreciated the love and care they provided her.Margie lived the last two and a half years of her life at Our Lady of Peace. There she renewed some old friendships and made many new ones. She was absolutely adored by the nurses, attendants, and staff there. Mom had a heart of gold and two of her often spoken words were "Thank you!""It takes a minute to find a special person, an hour to appreciate them, a day to love them, an entire life to forget them." Margie was one of those special persons and she leaves all that knew her a lifetime of memories to cherish.

A memorial service will be held at 1:30 p.m. on Thursday, September 15, 2016, at First United Methodist Church of Charlottesville with the Reverend Al Horton officiating. Visitation will follow.Interment at Riverview Cemetery will be private.In lieu of flowers, memorial donations may be made to First United Methodist Church, 101 E. Jefferson Street, Charlottesville, VA 22902.Condolences may be sent to the family at www.hillandwood.com.
