  • William Ijams was a member of a 13 man juror that ruled on July 21st, 1710 that Stephen Wright owed 400 lbs tobacco and Hezekiah Linicomb 480 lbs to the merchants Thomas Macnemara and John Mitchell of Annapolis.
  • MARYLAND COURT OF APPEALS 163

    By Virtue of A Writt Issued out of her Majestys Provinciall Court Coll
    William Holland Esqr Chief Justice to the Sheriff of Ann Arundell County
    to Summon twelve men of his ballywick to enquire what goods and Chatties
    Lands or tenements Thomas Macnemara and John Mitchell of the City of
    Annapolis and parish of St Anns in the County above said Gentlemen had on
    the thirteenth day of July last past or at any time after wee the Jurors Sub-
    scribed do make our return as followeth Vizt upon Information that one
    Steven Wright is indebted to the said Macknemara the Sume of four hun-
    dred pounds of tobacco and that Hezekiah Lincicomb is indebted to the said
    Macknemara the Sume of four hundred and Eighty pounds of tobaccoe which
    said Debt is all upon A Strict enquirey and information that wee can find
    of the above said Gentlemens estates If these be so deemed and so wee say all
    and give it under our hands and seales this 16th day of September 1710
    Jno Gresham Sher [Seale]
    John Lamb [seale] jno Davidge [seale]
    Samll Chambers [seale] Wm Ijams [seale]
    Jno Monat [seale] Antho Ruley [seale]
    Rob' Sanders [seale] Jno Duvall [seale]
    Cha: Ridgley [seale] Thos, Freeborne [seale]
    Robt Lisby [seale] Robt Johnson [seale]
