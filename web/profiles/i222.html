    Obituary
    obit May 7, 1973 Safford, Graham Co, Arizona 

    SAFFORD...Funeral services for Sheldon L. Ijams, 89, who passed away Saturday, May 5, in the Mountain View Nursing Home in Safford where he had been a patient for the past ten days, were conducted at 2 :00 p.m. Tuesday, May 8, in Caldwell's "Chapel of the Valley", with officers of Mt. Graham Lodge No. 24, I.O.O.F. officiating. Services began with the hymn "The Old Rugged Cross", followed by the opening portion of the Odd fellow services, with Marvin Imel serving as Noble Grand. The story of Mr Ijam's long and industrious life was given by his close friend, Ryder Ridgway. Chaplain for the services was T.J. Quinn. The closing hymn was "Abide With Me". Concluding service was in the family plot of the Safford Cemetery, where casket bearers were Mel Ijams, James Ijams, Dennis V. Shurtz, and Edward Morris. Complete arrangements were under the direction of the Caldwell Funeral Home of Safford. Mr Ijams was a native of Safford, being born April 25, 1884, and was the son of E.T. Ijams and Elizabeth Glaspy Ijams. As a young child, he moved with his parents to a ranch near Bowie, where he started his education. He attended the University of Arizona in Tucson before completing his education at Bliss Electrical School in Washington, D..C. As a young man he and his father installed the first telephone service to Tombstone, and later installed the lines to San Carlos. He lived some time in Florida and California before moving back to Tombstone where he operated the electric plant there for some 10 years before returning to Safford in 1936. He served on the O.P.A., or rationing board in Safford during World War II. After his retirement, he managed several rentals he owned around Safford. He was a member of the Mt Graham Lodge No 24, I.O.O.F. for some 35 years and was honored with a life membership two years ago.

    He is survived by his wife, Jessie, of Safford, and six sons, Harold and James O. both of Tucson, Edward T. of Oakland, Calif., Mel of Globe, Leslie of Phoenix, and Rev. Karl Phillip of South Weymouth, Mass; 16 grandchildren and eight great-grandchildren.
