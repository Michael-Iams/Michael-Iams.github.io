    Waynesburg, Nov. 9.- Mary Virginia Patterson Iams, 80, well known and highly respected Waynesburg woman, died at 7 o’clock this morning at her home on West Franklin Street, after an illness of several weeks.
    Mrs. Iams was born at Nineveh, April 29, 1858. She was a daughter of James and Elizabeth Spragg Patterson. She was married to G. P. Iams, Oct. 3, 1877. Her husband survives.
    On Oct. 3, 1927, they celebrated their golden wedding anniversary.
    Besides her husband, she leaves four children: Gail Iams Cowieson and James E. Iams, at home, and Hazel, wife of George Conner, of Dunns Station, and S. R Iams, of Kansas City Mo. There are eight grandchildren and one great-grandchild. One brother, Dr. F. I. Patterson, of Washington, also survives.
    Mr. And Mrs. Iams lived for a number of years at Graysville, moving in 1910 to Waynesburg, where they have since resided.
    Mrs. Iams has been an active member in the Methodist Episcopal Church, and was a member of the Ladies’ Aid Society and the Foreign and Home Missionary Society. For a number of years she was a teacher of the Women’s Adult Bible Class.
    Funeral services will be held Friday afternoon, Nov. 11, at 2 o’clock, at the home, in charge of her pastor, the Rev. W. Rufus Hofelt. Burial will be in the Green Mount Cemetery.
