    Re: DELILAH IJAMS,d/o VACHEL IJAMS
    Posted by: Roberta W. Iiames Date: July 02, 2000 at 05:52:41
    In Reply to: Re: DELILAH IJAMS,d/o VACHEL IJAMS by Donna Martin of 437

    I have a Vachel Ijams b. Jan. 1, 1759 in A.A.Co. MD and d. Feb. 20, 1833 in Florence, Lauderdale Co., ALA. he married first to Lilah Gaither she died in 1790 and this Vachel married second to Martha Gaither Cunningham on Sept. 19, 1791. Martha was b. Feb. 6, 1770 and the daughter of Joseph Cunningham, sr. and Anne Buntyn. They had 9 children.
    While he was living in A.A. Co., he substituted in the Militia for five months and was assigned to a compny commanded by a Frenchman LaVah. He enlisted as a Private in Capt. Watkins Co. of the Flying Camp about Oct. 1, 1776. He served three months as a Private in 1777 in Cap.Borgden's Co., Col. Dorseys Maryland reg. and in the battle of Germantown. He enlisted July 1782 in Maryland Militia but was transferred to Capt. Lavasche's Co. under Major Oxborough of the 4th Maryland Reg. and served five months. He was granted a furlough to return to his father's home in Prince George's Co. Md. because of a fever that he contracted in camp. He received a discharge on Dec. 11, 1781 which was turned over to the auditor of accounts so that he could get his pay.
    Sometime between the date of discharge and before July 1782, he removed to Rowan Co., N.C. and here he enlisted in Capt. Sharpe's Co., 18th NC Reg. commanded by Col. Lyter. He served as a sergeant until his discharge or furloughed near Charlestown, SC
    in 1782. His military records reveals all of the above and more. He and his wife settled in Tennessee and then moved to Rowan Co., N.C. remaining there about a year and moving to Lauderdale, Ala. where in died on Jan. 20, 1833. In 1833 his surviving children were William, Joseph, Pearson, Ann,
    Elizabeth [Hendren] and Peggy Edwards. The Vachel Ijams that you show b. 1770 is not the same Vachel Ijams as above. Roberta
