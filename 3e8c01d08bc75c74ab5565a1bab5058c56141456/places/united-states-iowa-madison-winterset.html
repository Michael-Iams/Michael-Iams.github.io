Winterset Madisonian, Winterset, Iowa
February 21, 1917, page 3

MICHAEL IIAMS

Michael Iiams, son of Jesse and Mary Iiams, was born in Maryland, March 18, 1833 and departed this life Feb. 10, 1917, aged 83 years, 10 months and 22 days. In 1835 his parents removed to Ohio, where they resided until he was 22 years old, when they came to Madison county and settled on the farm which has since been his home.

He was united in marriage to Miss Celista Blanchard on May, 1865. To this union were born 12 children, two of whom have preceded the father in death. He leaves to mourn his departure, his aged companion, 10 children, 47 grandchildren, 9 great grandchildren and a wide circle of friends.

The funeral was held at Madison Chapel church of Christ at 1 o'clock on Feb. 13th, conducted by Rev. White. Interment in Union cemetery.

Winterset Madisonian, Winterset, Iowa
March 15, 1922

CHARLES M. IAMS

Charles Melvin Iams was born May 30, 1868, in Lee township, Madison county, Iowa and died Feb. 18, 1922 at his home in Des Moines being 53 years and 9 months of age.

The deceased spent his boyhood days in Winterset and later returned to the same place after many years of service in the U. S. army. He was a Spanish-American war veteran. He enlisted at the age of 24 years, was wounded at the battle of San Juan hill and later honorably discharged. He re-enlisted, and was sent with his regiment to the Chinese boxer uprising and afterwards transferred to the Philippine Islands during the insurrection and was made one of Gov. W. H. Taft's body-guards. He retired from army life Sept. 1, 1905.

On July 7, 1905, Mr. Iams was united in the bonds of matrimony to Ida Mae Pomeroy, who survives him. We are glad to say that Mr. Iams was a Christian man. He united with the Winterset church, where his membership is until now. Early in his manhood, he had appetite for the calling of the ministry, and went to the LeGrand Bible school, to prepare his mind for this work.

He is survived by one brother, who was present at the funeral, which was held in Des Moines.

Note: Burial in the Glendale Cemetery at Des Moines.

Winterset Madisonian - November 21, 1945
Winterset, Iowa

ROY M. IMES DIED IN TRUCK CRASH

Two Madison county men were instantly killed in accidents during the past week. The first was a highway crash, and the second a farm machinery accident.

Roy M. Imes, St. Charles truck operator, was killed last Thursday afternoon on Highway 92 near Massena, when the stock truck in which he was riding crashed into a ditch. Mr. Imes was 49 years of age.

The truck was owned by Imes and driven by his nephew, Ivyl Imes, also of St. Charles. They were enroute to Omaha when the wreck occurred. The driver said that something broke suddenly in the driving mechanism of the vehicle, sending it careening into a ditch. The driver was not injured, but the elder Imes died almost instantly.

Mr. Imes is survived by his wife, Mrs. Marie Imes, and a foster son, both of St. Charles, and a brother, Burle Imes of Arvada, Colo.

(Burial was made in the St. Charles cemetery)

Lula Kirkland Dies In Chicago

Funeral Services Held on Tuesday at Macksburg Methodist Church

Mrs. Lula M. Kirkland of Winterset, a life-time resident of Madison county, died Friday, Dec. 15, at a hospital in Chicago, where she had been visiting. She was 76 years of age.

Mrs. Kirkland was born near Barney April 14, 1891. She was the former Lula Iiams, daughter of William and Lara Garrett Iiams. She was married at Winterset on Sept. 14, 1910, to William A. Kirkland. His death occurred in 1964. One son, Lyle, also preceded her in death.

For the last five years, her home had been in Winterset. She was a member of the Winterset Art center and the 60-Plus club.

She leaves three sons, Cecil Kirkland of Vallejo, Calif., Eugene (Pat) Kirkland of Coco Beach, Fla., Wendell Kirkland of Chicago; a daughter, Mrs. Ralph Pinckney of Iowa Falls, six grandchildren; and eight great grandchildren.

Funeral services were held Tuesday from the Macksburg Methodist church, conducted by the Rev. Robert Almquist. Burial was made in the Moon cemetery at Macksburg.

Services Tuesday for DORIS WADDELL
Funeral services were held Tuesday, June 30, 1981, at First Christian Church for Doris Eileen Bullock Waddell of Van Meter. Mrs. Waddell died Saturday, June 27, 1981, at the Coon Valley Gravel Pit in Polk County. She was 43 years old.

Winterset Madisonian, Winterset, Iowa
October 4, 2006

MILDRED BUSCH, Winterset

Mildred Burdette Busch, 97, died Sept. 29, 2006, at West Bridge Care & Rehabilitation in Winterset.

Mildred Busch was born April 25, 1909, in Lincoln, Neb., to Marshall and Josephine (Burdette) Iiams. She was united in marriage to Stanley Busch on May 26, 1940, in Cromwell. Mildred, a resident of the Creston and Winterset areas, taught home economics at several smaller schools, including Macksburg High School. She was a member of the Macksburg United Methodist Church, United Methodist Women, Madison County Farm Bureau Women, Friendly Neighbor Garden Club, and Orient-Macksburg PTA. She also was a 4-H leader.

She was preceded in death by her husband; a brother, Manly Iiams; and a sister, Lois Lawson.

She is survived by one son, Jim Busch of Winterset; three daughters, Karen White of Cedar Rapids, Sandra Murray of Jefferson City, Mo., and Linda Dwyer of Bella Vista, Ark.; and seven grandchildren and six great-grandchildren.

April 2, 2008

RUSSELL IIAMS, CEDAR RAPIDS

Russell W. Iiams, 67, of Cedar Rapids died March 28, 2008, at his home following a long illness.

Funeral services were held Tuesday, April 1, at Cedar Hills Community Church in Cedar Rapids with the Revs. Leonard Ranson and Kent Landhuis officiating. Burial was at Linwood Cemetery in Cedar Rapids. A memorial fund has been established in Russell’s name. Arrangements were handled by Murdoch-Linwood Funeral Home & Cremation Service in Cedar Rapids. Online condolences may be made at www.murdochfuneralhome.com.

Russell Iiams was born March 27, 1941, to Henry and Alice (Muck) Iiams of Peru. His marriage to Nanci Johnson took place May 9, 1964, in Peru. Russell was the owner of Mid-Western Exterminating Company for 38 years. His community involvement included coaching his children and others through the YMCA.

He was preceded in death by his parents; three brothers, Royal, Vernon and Raymond Iiams; and three sisters, Mary Kalianoff, Luellen Christensen and Doris Waddell.

He is survived by his wife, Nanci, of Cedar Rapids; children, Terry Iiams, Stacy Iiams and Dustin Iiams, all of Cedar Rapids; a sister, Evelyn Owens of Lorimor; and four grandchildren.
