    Note #1

    * George W. Imes asked for Stella's hand in marriage when she was 12 years old. Her parents told him to come back next year when she was 13 years old and that she was too young right now. He did and they got married when Stella was 13 and George was 28 years old.
    * George W. Imes was a cowboy, cowpuncher in Colorado about 1875.
    * Never drank or smoked.
    * Was in a saloon in Colorado to eat dinner, when he left to go out a Mexican followed him out to rob him and George W. Imes shot him from underneath his horse. He thought he best leave Colorado forever, so he jumped on his horse and went to Iowa.
    * In 1897 George W. took his kids, Verle C. Imes and Julian (G.W.) Imes and stepmother Margarette to Oklahoma and homesteaded 160 acres 15 miles out of Annadarko, Oklahoma.
    * George W. farmed and traded horses with the Kwioa and Tonkawa Indians.
    * George W. Imes was always suspected of having a little Indian blood in him by his son Verle Clair Imes because he spent so much time with them eating, etc.
    * George W. broke horses to ride and sell, and he put Verle C. Imes on horses at 7 and 8 years old that were still not fully broken. Verle on one occasion in Annadarko saw 2000 Kwioa Indians drawing their rations. On another occasion Verle leaving Annadarko on horseback to return to homestead was approached by two Kwioa youths on horseback, who wanted to race. Verle refused, fearing violence.
    * George W. Imes was killed bringing 2 ton of coal from Annadarko in a spring wagon. Mules were pulling the wagon spooked and runaway, the spring seat catapulted George into the air and he landed under wheels of wagon, crushing his chest. He crawled 3 miles to homestead, told what happened and passed away.
    * The two boys Verle and Julian Imes returned to their natural mother Stella Imes in Oelwein, Iowa.
    * Their stepmother Margarette went to Washington state.
    * It is suspected that changed his name from Iiams to Imes. We don't know when but all of his children were named Imes.
    * In the 1880 Census Report he was listed as 18 years old.
    * In the 1900 Census Report he listed his place of birth as Missouri?
    * In the 1900 Census his residence was listed as 430 Locuah Street, Des Moines, Iowa.
