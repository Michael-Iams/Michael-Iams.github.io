Richard Ijames Dead.
Richard D. Ijames died of asthma at his home on Walnut street at 5:30 o'clock Sunday afternoon. He was one of the oldest citizens of Owensboro and resided here continuously for more than fifty years. Mr. Ijames was born near Gallatin, Sumner county, Tenn., September 15, 1829, and grew to manhood there. In 1851 he removed with his father to Owensboro, and shortly thereafter he was married to Miss Ellen Lovell.
The deceased is survived by his wife and eight children, all of whom are grown. The funeral will be conducted from the home tomorrow morning at 10 o'clock and the remains will be interred in Elmwood.

Ruby Crabtree Ijames: Newspaper Obituary and Death Notice
Owensboro Messenger-Inquirer (KY) - Wednesday, November 9, 1994
Deceased Name: Ruby Crabtree Ijames
Ruby Crabtree Ijames, 81, of 1205 Werner Ave., Owensboro, died Tuesday, Nov. 8, 1994, at Owensboro-Daviess County Hospital. She was born in Daviess County and was a member of Temple Baptist Church and the Class of Ruth Sunday school class. She was the daughter of Remus and Alice Josephine Stone Crabtree. Her husband, Jasper Newton Ijames Jr., died in 1980. She was preceded in death by two sons, William Ijames and Larry Wayne Ijames.

Survivors include two daughters, Nellie Greer and Sheila Ijames, both of Owensboro; five sons and four daughters-in-law, Lee Tyler and Nancy Ijames of Louisville, James Robert and Brenda Ijames, Charles Francis and Barbara Ijames and Jasper N. Ijames, all of Owensboro, and Richard E. and Sherry Ijames of Philpot; 25 grandchildren; 15 great-grandchildren; and a sister, Myrtle Cavney of Indianapolis.

Services are at 11:30 a.m. Friday at James H. Davis Funeral Home. Burial in Brushy Fork Cemetery in Panther. Visitation is from noon to 8 p.m. Thursday and after 9 a.m. Friday at the funeral home. The family requests that expressions of sympathy take the form of donations to the Temple Baptist Church Building Fund, 1528 W. Ninth St., Owensboro, Ky. 42301. Memorial contribution envelopes are available at the funeral home.

Richard Denton IjamesObituaryNov 4, 1998Owensboro, Daviess, Kentucky, United States
Owensboro Messenger-Inquirer (KY) - Wednesday, November 4, 1998
Deceased Name: Richard D. Ijames
EVANSVILLE - Richard D. Ijames, 88, of Evansville died Monday, Nov. 2, 1998, at Welborn Baptist Hospital in Evansville. He served in the Army during World War II, where he was wounded and was an engineer, and was a member of East Riverside Chapel Pentecostal Church in Evansville. He retired as owner of Hottle House in 1975 after 31 years of service and was formerly employed by Servel, Glenmoor Distillery and the Kentucky Highway Department. His wife, Marietta Vanover Ijames, died in 1988.

Survivors include one grandchild; a sister, Suzanne I. Phillips of Owensboro; and several nephews and nieces.

Services are at 10 a.m. Thursday at Simpson-Meyer Funeral Home in Evansville. Burial will be in Park Lawn Cemetery in Evansville. Visitation is from 2 to 8 p.m. today at the funeral home.
