    Note #1

    Could Richard and his brother Thomas be these?

    http://www.genealogy.com/forum/surnames/topics/ijams/409/

    Richard Snowden continues to play a role in unraveling the Iiams clan. Granted it was a much smaller world in those days and the Snowdens were very wealthy land holders. Remember, we have Richard Snowden Sr. 1640-1711 as the emigrant. R. Snowden Jr.(1666-1720) purchased "Burgess Choice" of 300 acres on 4/19/1715. And R.S. III (1688-1763) inherited "Birmingham Manor" and all the lands (~10,000 acres) from his father in 1720. Isee a land dispute between Thomas Iiams and Richard Iiams over the deed to "New Birmingham" on 6/25/1796. Who were they?
    A Richard Snowden (either Jr. or III) bought the indenture for the Spurgeons who went on to Bedford County Pa. next door to William and Hugh Iiams documented in the 1780's, with the folk lore having this property being originally that of a Richard Iiams going back to 1730's. Spurgeon's Choice was patented in 1763, although the deed mentions the place already existed. I am more and more inclined to believe the Folk lore and which ever Richard Iiams this was, he connects the Bedford County/Allegany County Md. William Iams (~1755-1831) to the Anne Arundel County progenitors
