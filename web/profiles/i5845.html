    Obituary
    Doyle Keith Iiams,

    Age 89, of Eastview Drive, Fort Loramie, passed away of natural causes Tuesday evening, May 8, 2018, at the Heritage Manor Nursing Center in Minster, Ohio. He was born November 20, 1928, in Sidney, Ohio, to the late Urriel and Pearl (Griffis) Iiams. On June 13, 1953, at St. Michael Catholic Church in Fort Loramie, Doyle married Margaret (Toller) Iiams who survives. Also surviving are three children: Kurt Iiams of Fort Loramie, Pam & Chuck Cary of Defiance, Elaine & Chuck Ernst of Fort Loramie along with four grandchildren: Matt & Katie Ernst, Lauren & Ryan Grillot, Andy & Jen Cary and Brad Cary and five great-grandchildren: Aubrey and Eli Ernst, Vera, Henry and Oscar Grillot; sisters & brothers-in-law: Dorothy Laufer of Troy, Lester Rosengarten of West Milton, Helen Meyer of McCartyville, Norma Toller of Fort Loramie, Alvin & Charlotte Toller of Kettering as well as numerous nieces and nephews. He was preceded in death by one brother, Myron & Janice Iiams and several brothers & sisters-in-law: Frank & Virginia Toller, Paul Laufer, Mildred & Robert Fridley, Mary Rosengarten, Lawrence Meyer and Ed Toller.

    Mr. Iiams was a 1946 graduate of Sidney High School. He then went on to study engineering earning an AE degree at Tri-State University in Angola Indiana. Doyle served in the US Army Air Corps / Air Force immediately following WWII. He retired from Copeland Corp. of Sidney in 1992. He was Supervisor of Document Control and had previously been a long-time project engineer. Doyle was a member of St. Michael Catholic Church, the Fort Loramie American Legion and the Fort Loramie Youth Organization. He was a charter member of Fort Loramie Rescue Squad - Board of Directors and the GYM Committee. He was a past member of the Fort Loramie Kiwanis / Fort Loramie Community Service Club. Doyle served 33 years on the Village of Fort Loramie city council that included 8 years as mayor and 17 years on the Board of Public Affairs. He also served on the Shelby County Regional Planning Commission.

    Many years ago, Doyle coached "Pee Wee League" baseball. He was known for having whittled more than 140 model airplanes, authentic in both scale and detail. He was a loyal Woody Hayes - Buckeye Football fan. Over the years he also enjoyed bowling, fishing, golf and computer Solitaire.

    Mass of Christian Burial will be 10:30 AM, Monday, May 14, 2018, at St. Michael Church in Fort Loramie with Rev. Steven Shoup presiding. Full military honors provided by the American Legion will follow on the church plaza. A private committal service will take place at St. Michael Cemetery. Friends may call Sunday 3:00 to 7:00 PM and Monday 9:00 to 10:00 AM at Gehret Funeral Home in Fort Loramie. Memorials may be made to the Fort Loramie Rescue Squad or charity of donor's choice. Condolences may be expressed at www.gehretfuneralhome.com.
    Published in The Community Post on May 17, 2018
