    Harry L. Wickman Story

    Col. Harry L. Wickman, 82, a longtime resident of The Dalles, Died at the local hospital on Wednesday morning. He was born on Aug. 9 1905 to William A. and Pauline (Senecal) Wickman at the family home near Petersburg. He attended school at the lower Five Mile School, then at Petersburg and later moved to The Dalles and attended High School in The Dalles. After graduation from high school, he married Myrtle P. Byers on Dec. 2 1925. He began his military career in The Dalles in 1923 and served six years in the CMTC (Calvary unit) at Fort Lewis in Washington, and in 1923 joined the Oregon National Guard Unit Company H. When that unit was activated in 1924, he became an officer in the company and commanded it. Col. Wickman was a World War II veteran, serving overseas with the Ninth Air Force in England, France, Belguim, Holland, and Germany in 1944-1945. He won the bronze star, the Air Medal, the Belguim Defense medal. "Albert Prince of the Regents" and many campaign medals with accompanying combat stars. His service overseas was as an administrative executive officer with a P-30 Fighter group, the 474 Group, and Ninth Air Force he was then promoted to personnel office with the 19th Tactical Air Command and then to Colonel of the General Staff. After the war he served with the Fourth Air Force in San Francisco and was Inspector General. In all he served 32 years in the military and retired on Feb. 28 1957. He was a past member of the Sojourners, Heroes of '766 and was past president of The Dalles Downtown Kiwanis Club. He is also past president of the Old Wasco County Pioneer Association and a life member of the Order of Travelers, the military branch of the Masonic Lodge. He reached the sublime degree of Master Mason in 1927, later becoming a member of the York Rite bodies of Free Masonry and the Commandery and Order of Mystic Shrine Al Kader Lodge of Portland and the Camel Herders of the Shrine.Survivors include his wife, Myrtle; three daughters, Mavis Jane Ruddell, Shirley Ann Allen, and Evelyn Marie Wilkinson; five grandchildren four great grandchildren, one great great granddaughter, and several cousins, nephew and nieces. Burial at Parklawn Cemetery.
