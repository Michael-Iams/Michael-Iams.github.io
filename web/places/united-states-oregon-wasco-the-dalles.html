Charles Hugh KeelerDeathSep 5, 1867The Dalles, Wasco, Oregon, United States
Charles Keeler was a German born immigrant and was the Marshall of The Dalles, Oregon.

In 1867, when his wife Mary was 7-months pregnant with her first child Emma, Charles was shot and killed after arresting a soldier from the Army's 23rd Infantry for disorderly conduct. While escorting the suspect to jail, Marshal Keeler and a deputy were approached by a group of eight to ten soldiers who demanded his release. When Marshal Keeler refused to release the soldier. At first, he was able to persuade the soldiers to peaceably return to their camp. But the soldiers only pretended to return. Shortly thereafter, shots were fired. Keeler and his deputy approached the soldiers, identifying himself as “Marshall of Dale City.” A soldier yelled, “halt, or I’ll shoot.” Keeler calmly approached the soldier saying “oh no, you won’t shoot.” Then the soldier fired his musket into Keeler’s chest from only ten feet away. Keeler was taken to a nearby doctor, but bled to death within ninety minutes. His last words were reportedly; “what will my poor wife and children do?” With a young child to care for, Mary married Charles’ best friend, Frederick Wickman less than a year later.

Mary Elizabeth SchusterObituaryMay 26, 1917The Dalles, Wasco, Oregon, United States [2144]
The Dalles, Or., May 26

Mrs. Mary Elizabeth Wickman, who died recently at her home near The Dalles, was one of the earliest of Oregon's pioneers. She crossed the plains when a girl of 13 years and resided in Oregon continuously until her death. She was married to Frederic Wickman, July 1, 1868, and eight[sic] children were born to them, Elizabeth, Frederic, William, Henry, Ella, Francis and Leo. The latter died in infancy. Mr. Wickman died a number of years ago.

Besides here children, Mrs. Wickman leaves two brothers, William Schuster of Goldendale, Wash. and Charles Schuster of Seattle and two sisters, Mrs. Eliza Schanno of The Dalles and Mrs. Rose Maskelyn of Walla Walla.
