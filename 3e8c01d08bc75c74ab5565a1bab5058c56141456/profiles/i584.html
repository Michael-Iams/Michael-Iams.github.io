    ObituaryTranscribed by Laura Nelle Poland Mallett on March 09, 2006 [72]
    William W. Iams, 65, of 80 Cleveland Road, Washington, formerly of Wind Ridge, died at 4:57 PM Monday, Aug. 5, 1985, in the Oakland Veteran’s Administration Hospital, Pittsburgh, following surgery.
    He was born April 12, 1920, in Washington, a son of William B. And Annie Green Iams.
    Mr. Iams was employed by Universal Cyclops. He retired in 1971 because of poor health.
    He was Protestant.
    Mr. Iams was a member of Edwin Scott Linton Post No. 175, Washington; Washington Fraternal Order of Eagles; the Anawanna Sportsmen’s Club and the Independent Club.
    He was a US Army veteran of World War ll.
    On Nov. 7, 1953, in Claysville, he married Margaret Cumberledge, who survives.
    Surviving in addition to his wife are one son, Myron Lee Iams of Washington; two daughters, Donna J. Manning of Eighty Four, Mrs. David “Judith Ann” Critchfield of Pittsburgh; four brothers, Elmer Iams of Flagstaff, Arizona, James Iams, Dennis Iams and Oscar Iams, all of Washington; two sisters, Mrs. Martha Dille, Mrs. Emma Moore, both of Washington and four grandchildren.
    Deceased are three sisters, Margaret Wise, Ethel Glendenings, and Leona Iams; one brother, Charles, who died in infancy.
    More:
    Friends of William W. Iams of 80 Cleveland Road, Washington, formerly of Wind Ridge, who died Monday were received from 7-9 PM Tuesday and will be received from 2-4 and 7-9 PM Wednesday in the Hunnell and Barnhill Funeral Home Inc., 585 Fayette Street, Washington, where services will be held at 11 AM Thursday, Aug. 8, with Dr. George W. Hall officiating. Interment in Baddock Cemetery, Graysville. The family asks that those wishing to make donations make them to the Southwestern Pennsylvania Chapter of the American Heart Association, 298 East Beau Street, Washington, 15301.
