    Long Wedded Iowan. 

    Washington, Ia., Dec. 24 - Mr. and Mrs. John Iams of Washington county are the longest married of any couple in the state. If they survive until next September they will have been wedded seventy-two years. Mr. Iams was born in Greene county, Pennsylvania, April 13, 1809, and his wife, who is a native of Washington county in the same state, is only two months his junior. Soon after their marriage they removed to Licking and then to Newark, Ohio, but they left that state in 1838 to come to Iowa. They have since resided here.

    John Iams was born in Green county PA. He was the son of Richard Iams and Mary Schidler Iams and married Sarah McVey in 1828. After his father was killed they moved on to Licking county, Ohio near Newark where their first child Richard was born.They later moved to Delaware County Ohio where he worked in a Tannery. In the fall of 1838 he moved to Washington County Iowa and settled near Dutch Creek. He walked to the Government Land Office at Fairfield and bought 40 acres of land for $50.He said he borrowed the money from a \"Dutchman\". There were only two or three families in the Dutch Creek area at the time. He lived first in a railpen covered with rails and brush - then a log house. Next a larger log house and later a frame addition. Finally a farm house was built with a walk in basement. He continued to acquire more land until he had a quarter section.. The Indians lived around them for the first ten years and they traded with them.. He saw the country develop from a wilderness of grass grown plains into prolific fields... Sarah (Sally) Singmaster lived with them until she married Wm Knox and then they moved in and farmed at Dutch Creek. They later moved to Valley farm and John Iams went to live with them there after the death of his wife in 1903. He died in Dutch Creek Iowa on March 9, 1905.
