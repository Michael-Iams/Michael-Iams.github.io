Donald George IiamesObituarySep 15, 1952Lima News, Lima, Allen, Ohio, United States
Donald George Iiames, 23, of 711 St. Johns Avenue, died at 5:55 p.m. Sunday in St. Rita's hospital from injuries received at 3 a.m. Friday when a car he was driving overturned about a mile south of Cridersville on Route 25. He was found lying unconscious near the car by a passing motorist. The car, owned by Ernest Morris, 143 E. Eureka Street, was demolished, according to state highway patrolmen.

Highway patrolmen at first were unable to establish Iiames' identity until a fingerprint check by Waldo Stevick, Lima Police Identification officer, was made while Iiames lay critically injured in the hospital.

Dr. T. J. Talbott, Allen County coroner, said Iiames died of a brain concussion and hemorrhages. He ruled the death accidental.

A native of Lima, Iiames was employed as a dock helper at the Duff Truck Lines, Inc., Broadway and Vine Street. He served in the U.S. Army from July, 1946, to May, 1947.

Surviving are his widow, Geraldine; a son, Donald Lee, and a daughter, Joann Lynn, both at home; his mother, Mrs. Edna Iiames, Palm Beach, Fla., and four brothers, Jack, Franklin, Terry and Michael, all of Palm Beach.

Arrangements, in charge of Davis-Miller and Son funeral home, are incomplete.

(published in The Lima News, Monday, September 15, 1952)
