  • Company H.
    Captain William E. Booe's Partisan Rangers; enlisted in Mocksville and Farmington, Davie County beginning July 8, 1862.

    The battalion was made up of North Carolina and Virginia companies. It never completed its organization because NC companies objected to serving with Virginia companies.

    Officially known as 63rd Regiment NC Troops (5th Regiment NC Cavalry)

  • Volume 5
    page 341

    * Ijames, James D. Sergeant
    Resided in Davie County and was by occupation a farmer prior to enlisted in Davie County at age 20, April 21, 1861. Mustered in as a Corporal. Present or accounted for until captured at Williamsburg, Virginia on or about May 6, 1862,. Exchanged and rejoined the company prior to November 1, 1862. Wounded at Fredericksburg, Virginia December 13, 1862. Present or accounted for until wounded in thigh at Chancellorsville, Virginia, May 3, 1863. Rejoined the company in July-August 1863, and was promoted to Sergeant on Sept 1, 1863. Present or accounted for until paroled at Appomattox Court House, Virginia, April 9, 1865.

    * Ijames, Mathew N., Corporal
    Resided in Davie County where he enlisted at age 19, August 6, 1861 Mustered in as a Private. Present or accounted for until wounded and captured at Williamsburg, Virginia, on or about May 6, 1862. Hospitalized at Washington, D.C., until transferred to Old Capitol Prison, Washington, June 27, 1962. transferred to Aiken's Landing, James River, Virginia, where he was received August 5, 1862, for exchange. Rejoined the company prior to November 1, 1862, and was promoted to Corporal on December 6, 1862. Present or accounted for until captured at Falling Waters, Maryland, July 14, 1863. Confined at Point Lookout, Maryland, until paroled and transferred to City Point, Virginia, where he was received March 20, 1864, and present or accounted for through October, 1864. Paroled at Salisbury in 1865.

Company A, 4th Regiment, NC Senior Reserves.

Ijames, William J. Private
Born on or about April 8, 1815, in the part of Rowan county that later became Davie County. Was by occupation a farmer prior to enlisting at Mocksville at age 49, July 11, 1864, for the war. No further records.


The 4th Infantry Regiment Senior Reserves was also known as the 73rd Infantry Regiment North Carolina Troops. It was organized in early 1865 and participated in the Carolinas campaign. It surrendered as part of the Army of TN at Durham Station, Orange Co, NC 04/26/1865. The 4th Reserves do not appear in the Official Records.
