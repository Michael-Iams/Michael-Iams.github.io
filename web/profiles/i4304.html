    Company A, 4th Regiment, NC Senior Reserves.

    Ijames, William J. Private
    Born on or about April 8, 1815, in the part of Rowan county that later became Davie County. Was by occupation a farmer prior to enlisting at Mocksville at age 49, July 11, 1864, for the war. No further records.


    The 4th Infantry Regiment Senior Reserves was also known as the 73rd Infantry Regiment North Carolina Troops. It was organized in early 1865 and participated in the Carolinas campaign. It surrendered as part of the Army of TN at Durham Station, Orange Co, NC 04/26/1865. The 4th Reserves do not appear in the Official Records.

    Hi John, I am looking for information on Brice W. Ijams a son of John & Arina Worthington Ijams.
    Brice was born about 1761 in Md and died between 1820-1822 in Rowan Co., N. C. He had a son James Monroe Ijams b. Nov. 21, 1792-1798 in Clarksville Twp., Davie Co., N.C. and he married Lottie Stonestreet.
    Now, Brice married Betsey Anderson March 31, 1803 in Rowman Co., N. C. & they had three children born between 1804 & 1810. Evidently Betsey died and Brice married Mary J. Polly Smoot. At least all the genealogies that I have seen indicate Smoot however, Mary J. Polly was the daughter of John Hawkins and Nancy Anne Jones. John Hawkins died and Nancy married Alexander Smoot. He the will of Alexander, he names his step-children one of which is Mary J. Polly.
    Anyway, if Brice had a son b. 1792-1798, he had to have been married prior to marrying Betsey Anderson. When you consider Brice's age 1761 and the date of his first child 1792, it is 30 years. Since the marriage date of Betsey & Brice is after 1803, it doesn't seem likely that the marriage to Betsey was his first.
    I believe that Brice & Mary J. Polly Hawkins Smoot had a son named William Jackson Ijames b. May 28, 1822 in Clarksville Twp., Davie Co., N. C. I believe that this William married 4 times: SallyBaity Jan. 20, 1843 in Davie Co., Minerva Spry Aug. 4, 1848 and Mary Miller m. Fe.b 22, 1860 and 4th Elizabeth Anne Owen Feb. 19, 1861.
    What I am really interested in is whether or not you have information about Brice and his son James Monroe Ijams?
