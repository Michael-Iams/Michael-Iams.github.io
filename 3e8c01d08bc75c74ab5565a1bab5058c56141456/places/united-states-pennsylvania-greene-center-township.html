Samuel IamsBiography1888History of Greene County, Pennsylvania, Center Township, Greene, Pennsylvania, United States [106]
SAMUEL IAMS, retired farmer, Harvey’s, Penn., was born in Washington County, Penn., April 8, 1817. His parents, John and Anna (Coulson) Iams, were natives of Washington County, where they were married and remained through life. Mr. Iams died in December, 1866, and Mrs. Iams in November, 1886. They were the parents of five children, of whom three are living. Samuel was united in marriage, October 29, 1840, with Nancy Grimes, who was born in Greene County, August 15, 1817. Her parents were Peter and Mary (Sherwin) Grimes, deceased. The former was a native of this county, and the latter of Baltimore, Maryland. Mr. and Mrs. Samuel Iams are the parents of seven children, of whom five are living-Dr, John T., of Waynesburg, Penn.; G. P.; Ida, wife of Byron Braddock; Carrie, wife of James B. Throckmorton; and Samuel S. The deceased are Mary A. and Cordelia. Mr. Iams is a mill-wright by trade, which he followed for many years. He subsequently engaged in farming and stock raising, and owns about 420 acres of land in Greene County. Mrs. Iams and family are members of the Methodist Episcopal Church.

History of Greene County, Pennsylvania
Samuel P. Bates
Nelson, Rishforth & Co., Chicago. 1888

Samuel IamsDeathAug 29, 1894Center Township, Greene, Pennsylvania, United States [12]
Another Esteemed Citizen Gone

Samuel Iams, Esq., died at his residence on West High Street, Tuesday at 4 PM. He had been dangerously ill for some time, his condition being such that friends could not hope for recovery.

Mr. Iams was in his 78th year, having been born April 8, 1817. His parents resided near Hackney's Washington County. In early life he learned the trade of mill-wright under his father which he followed for a number of years. After his marriage he located at Mt. Morris, this county, residing there for about ten years. He then purchased a tract of land in Center township, about a mile and one-half east of Harvey's "or Graysville" during the year 1850. This he put in an excellent state of improvement, erecting a fine commodious dwelling, probably one of the best farm residences to be seen at that time. His homestead, contained nearly 500 acres is one of the most desirable in the county. In all his pursuits and investments Mr. Iams was remarkably successful. For about thirty years he was associated with Samuel Harvey, Esq., in buying wool and during that period they purchased nearly all the wool produced in the western part of the county. Their business associations led to a lifetime friendship which was never marred by the slightest jar or difference, Four years ago Mr. Iams purchased the residence adjoining that of Mr, Harvey and removed to Waynesburg the latter having become a resident of this place a few years previous. Deceased was a man who took a prominent part in all public enterprises and did much toward the improvement and advancement of the county. He always bore the sincerest respect of his friends and neighbors and sustained an unquestionable character for honor and integrity. He was an upright citizen and known for his fairness in all business transactions. He was a stockholder and director in the First National Bank of Waynesburg, and after it's close became a stockholder and director in the Farmers and Drovers National Bank, retaining the directorship until his health failed. In politics he was a steadfast Republican being a strong believer in the principles of the party.

He was united in marriage October 29, 1840, to Miss Nancy Grimes and they enjoyed a period of almost 54 years of unbroken happiness together. Mrs. Iams is but four months his junior. To them were born a family of seven children, five of whom survive: Dr. John T. Iams, of this place, G. P. Iams, S. S. Iams and Mrs. Byron Braddock, of Harvey's and Mrs. James B. Throckmorton, of Franklin Township. Two daughters are deceased, Mrs. Mary Buckingham and Sarah H., who died young. A brother of the deceased, John Iams, resides at Jacksonville.

The sincerest sympathy of many friends is extended to the family in their bereavement. The funeral will be held at his late residence here today at 10:AM. Services will be conducted by Rev. Edgell, assisted by the other minister of town. Interment will be in Green Mount Cemetery.
