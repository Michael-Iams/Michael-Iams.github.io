    1839 Tax List for Washington Township, Greene County
    Transcribed by Debbie Ovechka

    Continuing with the earliest tax lists for each township in Greene County, we have herewith the Washington Township list for the year 1839. Washington Township was established in 1838 and comprised land formerly in Morgan, Morris and Franklin Townships. The assessor was Christian J. Parker and occupations were not identified; however, most were farmers.
    Previous townships listed have been Aleppo (1818), Center (1820), Cumberland (1798), Dunkard (1809), Franklin (1809), Greene (1797), Jackson (1841), Jefferson (1800), Marion (1836), Monongahela (1830), Morgan (1800), Morris (1800), Perry (1843) and Richhill (1804).

    Note #1

    Thomas Iams is proven to be born in Maryland by the 1880 census entry for his son, Thomas Iams Jr. (Morris Twp., Greene County, Pennsylvania), in which he states that his father was born in Maryland.

    Will of Thomas Iams (Greene County Will Book, p. 171, no. 1067). Note: this will was difficult to read, primarily because of a paragraph written across the will itself, as described below. I have transcribed it as faithfully as I am able -- misspellings and all -- but some words I just couldn't make out.

    In the name of God Amen. I Thomas Iams of Washington Township Greene County and State of Pennsylvania being sick and weak in body but of sound mind memory and understanding praised be God for it and considering the certainty of Death and the uncertainty of the time therefore (?) and to the end I may be the better prepaired to leave the world whenever it shall please God to call me hence do therefore make and declare this my last Will and Testament in manner following that is to say first and principally I commit my soul into the hands of Almighty God my Creator hoping for my pardon and remission of all my sins and to enjoy everlasting happinessin the (illegible) Kingdom through Jesus Christ my Savior my body I commit to the earth at the Discretion of my executors hereinafter mentioned and named.

    I will that all my Just debts as shall be by my owing at my death together with my funeral expenses and charges touching the proving or otherwise concerning this my will shall in the first place out of my personal estate and effects be fully paid and satisfied and from and at the payment thereof and subject thereto then my will is that all of the residue of my estate shall remain in the hands and for the use of my loving wife Mary during her natural life if she shall so long remain my widow and at her death I will and bequeath to my son Dennis two hundred Dollars to be paid in two years after the death of my wife or if she shall depart this life before my son Thomas arives at the age of (illegible) the above mentioned two hundred Dollars shall not be paid. (Illegible) after Thomas is twenty one years old and at the death of my wife I will and bequeath all of my real and personal estate to my son Thomas. (Illegible) and bequeath to my daughter Elizabeth wife of George Smith one hundred Dollars to be paid to him in one year after Dennuce (NOTE: should this read "Dennis'?") two hundred dollars (illegible) and I will and bequeath to my daughter Catharine one hundred Dollars to be paid in one year after Elizabeth's and I will and bequeath to my daughter Mary one hundred Dollars to be paid in one year after Catherine's and I will and bequeath to my daughter Hannah one hundred Dollars to be paid in one year after Mary's and I will and bequeath to my daughter Rachel one hundred Dollars to be paid in one year after Hannah's and I will and bequeath to my daughter Charity one hundred Dollars to be paid in one year after Rachel's. My will and (illegible) is that my sone Thomas for and in consideration of getting the land other property is to pay all the rest of the legatees and I make and ordain my wife Mary Exetrix of this my last will and testament.

    Thomas (his mark) Iams (seal)

    Signed, sealed and acknowledged by the testator in the presents of the subscribers as his last will and testament April 23, 1841

    Otho Iams
    Benjamin Miller
