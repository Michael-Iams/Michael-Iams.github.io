    1826: Samuel Lord and George Washington Taylor open the first Lord & Taylor store at 47 Catherine Street. 1853: Lord & Taylor opens a new store at 255-261 Grand Street. It is the first store in New York City to use a large glass-domed central rotunda to light its interior. 1860: Lord & Taylor opens a new store on the corner of Broadway and Grand Street. It is one of the first stores in New York City to have a working passenger elevator. 1863: Armed employees, barricaded with cases of merchandise successfully defend the Grand Street store from looting mobs during the Civil War draft riots.

    The E. S. Ridley Department Store building survives today on Grand Street and Orchard Street, right in the middle of Delancey’s old square and in the midst of blocks choked with tenements. Ridley opened 1849, just before the German “wave.” In 1862, a horse drawn railroad line connected Grand Street with ferry terminals allowing shoppers from Brooklyn to easily shop Grand Street, creating an east-west shopping district that was Broadway’s closest rival. Ridley’s expanded to take up the entire block in 1883, just at the start of Eastern European and Russian “waves.”
