    History of the Thirtieth Regiment Ohio Volunteer Infantry : from its organization, to the fall of Vicksburg, Miss. (1863)

    http://archive.org/download/historyofthirtie00brin/historyofthirtie00brin.pdf

    Company K was organized by Captain Wm. H. Ijams, at Caldwell, Noble County, Ohio, during the early weeks of August, 1861.  The men were assembled amid great fanfare and given a hearty farewell, then proceeded to march a distance of one hundred and twenty-three miles to Camp Chase, near Columbus Ohio, where it arrived on the twenty-second day of August.  The company was mustered into the service of the United States on August 26, 1861 by Captain Howard Stambury.  The original muster roll bears the names of three commissioned officers, thirteen non- commissioned officers, and fifty-eight privates from Noble County Ohio. It is of interest to note that Company K was originally assigned to the 31st Ohio Regiment, but shortly afterwards was transferred to the 30th Ohio Regiment.
