Perry Co. Woman Dies After Crqsh

New Lexington

Mrs. Garnett L. Fauley, 57, of New Lexington Route 1, and employee of Bryant Heating Company here, died Sunday evening as a result of injuries sustained in a one - car accident at 6:19 p.m. on U.S. 22 three miles east of Lancaster.

Mrs. Fauley was pronounced dead shortly after 8 p.m. at Fairfield County Hospital in Lancaster.

Fairfield County sheriffs deputies reported Mrs. Fauley was eastbound on U.S. 22 when her car skidded off the roadway on a curve. The vehicle went into a ditch and struck a telephone pole. Deputies said the victim was pinned in the car more than a hour before she could be freed.

Born June 15, 1914, in Perry County, Mrs. Fauley was daughter of John and Sarah McDaniel Imes. She had been employed at Bryant 16 years and was a member of the New Lexington Baptist Church and the Perry County Women's Bowling League.

Surviving are here husband, John Fauley; a son, Ronald Byrd of Chicago, Ill.; two daughters, Mrs. Ralph (Kathryn) Dupler and Miss Anita Fauley, both of Columbus; a brother, Sherman Imes of Columbus; and five grandchildren.

Services will be held at 1:30 p.m. Wednesday at the Baptist Church with Rev. James Allison officiating. Burial will be in Greenlawn Memory Gardens.

Friends may call after 2 p.m. today at Chute-Wiley Funeral home here.

CALDWELL -- Helen Louise Blake, 92, lifelong resident of Caldwell , and a resident of Mandeville, La., for the past two years, died Friday, March 7, 2003, at Lakeview Regional Hospital, Mandeville.

She was a homemaker, member of Daughters of the American Revolution, Women's Literary Guild, Noble County Historical Society and Sharon Grange. She was preceded in death by her husband, Robert C. Blake a daughter, Rebecca Sheets and a brother, Thomas Bliss Iams.

Surviving are a son, Robert C. (Diane) Blake II of Mandeville four grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.
