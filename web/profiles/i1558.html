    Oath of Allegiance

    I do solemnly, in the presence of Almighty God, swear and declare that I
    will faithfully and sincerely support the Constitution of the United States,
    and obey all laws thereof, and will discontinue opposition thereto, except
    by way of petition and remonstrance, and all attempts to resist, obstruct,
    or illtreat the officers of the United States in the execution of their
    respective duties, so help me God.

    Each person, in taking this oath, was required to subscribe his name
    thereto, as well as sign the following pledge:

    In pursuance of the oath hereto annexed, I do hereby engage and associate to
    and with all others who may subscribe these presents to countenance and
    protect the officers of the United States in the execution of their duties
    according to law, and to discover and bring to justice all persons who may
    be concerned, directly or indirectly, in illegally hindering or obstructing
    the said officers, or any of them, in the execution of their duty, or in
    doing any manner of violence to them, or either of them. In witness of all
    which I have hereunto subscribed my hand the day and year opposite my name.

    IAMS/IIAMS, Thomas (S-8751)

    Belmont Co., OH, 10 Mar 1834
    Thomas IAMS, a resident of Richland Twp., Belmont Co., OH, aged 79 years on 26 Dec
    1833, volunteered under Capt. Richard STRINGER in the spring of the year preceding the battle
    or rout of Gen. WAYNE, at Piolen (typed letter indicates this was in 1776), he volunteered in Ann Arundel
    Co., MD, was discharged at the end of 3 weeks. In the late summer of the same year
    he volunteered under Capt. Richard STRINGER, in Ann Arundel Co., MD, was discharged after 2 months.
    In the spring of the year that Gen. WAYNE, was defeated at Piolen (Rolen?) (1777) he volunteered in the
    same company, served 2 weeks. In the summer or fall of the same year
    he volunteered for 3 months, served under capt. Richard STRINGER in Ann Arundel Co., MD.
    He was born in Ann Arundel Co., MD in 1754; he resided in that county when he volunteered,
    and lived there until about 40 years ago when he moved to Bethlehem Twp., Washington Co., PA,
    where he resided until 5 years ago when he moved to Richland Twp., Belmont Co., OH. He is
    acquainted with Henry MEEKS and George MEEKS in this neighborhood.

    Gary, IN, 1 Jan 1924
    Letter from R. B. WARREN, on behalf of his wife, whose great grandfather was Thomas
    IJAMS/IIAMS/IAMS, and who was a Sergeant in the MD army. Thomas IAMS had at least
    two brothers, Richard and William.
